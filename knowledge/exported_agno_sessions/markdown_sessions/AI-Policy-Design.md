# A first principles guide to ethical AI policy for Future's Edge

## Why start from first principles?

First principles thinking means stripping away assumptions and industry boilerplate to ask: *what do we actually believe, and why?* For Future's Edge — a youth-led movement built on decentralised trust, ethical incentives, and human-centred design — this matters enormously. An AI policy copied from a corporate template would contradict your deepest values. The goal is to build something that is *authentically yours*, then make it exemplary. ## Step 1: Anchor everything to your "why"

Before writing a single policy clause, revisit your non-negotiables. From your foundation documents, Future's Edge already holds a clear position: [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_134a7727-7089-45f0-be92-2776f84fd6ba/6b2aab65-549d-45c5-a576-40e8e2e07c01/policy-framework.md)

- **Trust is the currency** — all decisions, resource allocations, and interactions must be transparent and publicly auditable
- **Human-centred design is the method** — empathise before you define, define before you ideate
- **Ethical technology is the standard** — you stand *against* opaque decision-making and exploitative incentives

Your first principles question for AI is therefore: **"Does this AI use build trust, or erode it?"** Every policy clause flows from that single test.

***

## Step 2: Empathise with your stakeholders

True to your human-centred approach, before drafting policy you need to deeply understand the people affected. Map at least four groups:

- **Youth members** — worried about AI replacing their skills, curious about opportunity, sensitive to fairness and being heard
- **Mentors and educators** — concerned about authenticity of learning, accountability, and not being made redundant
- **Partner organisations and clients** — need confidence that AI-assisted outputs are trustworthy and attributable
- **The broader public and future members** — watching whether Future's Edge lives its values or just performs them

Australia's own AI ethics framework describes this as ensuring AI systems "respect human rights, diversity and the autonomy of individuals". For Future's Edge, autonomy and youth agency are especially sacred values. [finance.gov](https://www.finance.gov.au/government/public-data/data-and-digital-ministers-meeting/national-framework-assurance-artificial-intelligence-government/implementing-australias-ai-ethics-principles-government)

## Step 4: Design your governance model

Governance is where policy becomes real. Given Future's Edge's DAO architecture and blockchain-based transparency, you are ahead of most organisations. Structure your AI governance in three layers: [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_134a7727-7089-45f0-be92-2776f84fd6ba/8ec14bb2-b844-4ca6-9264-0b225c2adf1f/02-Strategy-Info.md)

**Layer 1 — Community constitution**
Enshrine the above principles in the Future's Edge constitution so they are member-governed, not just leadership-declared. This is non-negotiable for a trust-first DAO. [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_134a7727-7089-45f0-be92-2776f84fd6ba/6b2aab65-549d-45c5-a576-40e8e2e07c01/policy-framework.md)

**Layer 2 — Use case register**
Maintain a public, auditable register of every AI tool used within Future's Edge — what it does, who owns it, what data it uses, and what human oversight exists. Australia's updated AI policy (December 2025) now mandates this for government agencies; Future's Edge should adopt it voluntarily as a signal of leadership. [dta.gov](https://www.dta.gov.au/articles/ai-policy-update-strengthening-responsible-use-across-government)

**Layer 3 — Impact assessment before deployment**
Before any new AI tool is adopted, complete a lightweight impact assessment that asks: Does this serve our members' autonomy? Does it create or erode trust? Who is accountable? This mirrors the AI impact assessment framework now required by the Australian Public Service. [dta.gov](https://www.dta.gov.au/articles/ai-policy-update-strengthening-responsible-use-across-government)

## Step 5: Create your "AI for good" behavioural standards

Policy without behaviour change is wallpaper. Translate your principles into concrete norms for how Future's Edge members and staff actually *use* AI day-to-day:

- **Label AI-assisted work** — any content, decision, or output materially shaped by AI is disclosed clearly

- **Community reporting** — members can flag AI misuse through the existing dispute resolution system, anonymously if needed [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_134a7727-7089-45f0-be92-2776f84fd6ba/6b2aab65-549d-45c5-a576-40e8e2e07c01/policy-framework.md)

## Step 6: Make it a living document

The best ethical AI frameworks are not static. Australia's government policy explicitly commits to evolving as "the technology changes, leading practices emerge". For Future's Edge, the DAO governance model is your superpower here — build in a community review cycle (e.g., annually), allow members to submit policy amendments as proposals, and publish version history publicly. [digital.gov](https://www.digital.gov.au/ai/ai-in-government-policy)

## The single test to carry forward

Every AI decision Future's Edge faces — from adopting a new tool to automating a governance process — can be filtered through one question derived from your founding values: [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_134a7727-7089-45f0-be92-2776f84fd6ba/a1250f6f-53b3-49d0-8938-a919fa642bbb/01-Foundation-documents.md)

> **"Does this build trust, protect human agency, and make our members more capable — or does it undermine any of these?"**

If the answer is yes to all three, proceed. If not, redesign or reject. That discipline, applied consistently and transparently, is what makes a role model.
