### Our Approach: The Task Suitability Framework

We've developed a structured framework—validated across 10+ industries—that evaluates any task on 6 dimensions:

1. **Technical Automability** — Clear inputs/outputs, objective quality metrics  
2. **Current AI Capability** — What can frontier models actually do today?  
3. **Human-Necessary Factors** — Requires empathy, ethics, novel judgment?  
4. **Risk Tolerance** — What happens if AI makes a mistake?  
5. **Volume & Frequency** — How often does this task happen?  
6. **Strategic Value** — Does this create competitive advantage?

**In the workshop, we apply this framework collaboratively with your team**—scoring actual tasks from your workflows, identifying patterns, and building consensus.

**You leave with:**
- ✅ Clear classification: Automate fully / Augment with AI / Keep human

## SERVICE 3: Agentic Workflow Redesign

### Hero Section

**Headline:**  
**Automating Broken Processes Just Gives You Faster Failure.**  
**Let's Redesign for How Work Should Flow.**

**Subheadline:**  
We map your current workflow empirically, benchmark AI capabilities against each task, and redesign the entire process for optimal human-AI collaboration. Not automation. Transformation.

**CTA:** [Explore Workflow Transformation]

---

### The Challenge

**Your team adopted tools. Productivity didn't improve. Why?**

Because most "digital transformation" is just **automation of existing inefficiency**.

**The pattern:**
1. Manual process is slow and error-prone
2. Leadership says "let's automate this"
3. You implement new tools
4. Tools encode the existing bad process
5. You're still slow, just with more software

**Examples we see constantly:**
- Approval workflows with 7 steps (6 are unnecessary theater)
- Reports generated manually because "that's how we've always done it"
- Information copied between 3 systems because they don't integrate
- Work sitting in queues waiting for availability of specific people
- Quality problems caught late because verification happens at the end

**The cost:** You invested in tools, process didn't improve, team is frustrated, and leadership questions if "this AI thing" even works.

---

### The Truth: Tools Don't Fix Bad Workflows. Design Does.

**What if you could:**
- ✓ Eliminate 40-60% of process steps that exist only for coordination  
- ✓ Reduce cycle time by 50-70% by removing bottlenecks  
- ✓ Improve quality through automated verification at every step  
- ✓ Free humans to do high-judgment work instead of moving information around  
- ✓ Create processes that get smarter with use through captured learnings

**That's what agentic workflow redesign delivers.**

---

### Our Approach: Human-AI Workflow Design

**Phase 1: Empirical Current-State Mapping (Week 1-2)**

We don't ask how you *think* work happens. We observe how it *actually* happens:
- Workflow observation and screen recording studies
- Task decomposition at atomic level (what are the actual information transformations?)
- Dependency mapping (what blocks what? Where do handoffs fail?)
- Time and cost analysis (what's the total burden?)

**Phase 2: AI Capability Benchmarking (Week 2-3)**

For each task, we test:
- Can AI do this today? (We run actual experiments, not hypotheticals)
- At what quality level? (We measure, not estimate)
- What are the failure modes? (What goes wrong and how often?)
- Where does human judgment add unique value?

**Phase 3: Future-State Workflow Design (Week 3-5)**

We redesign the workflow from first principles:
- Remove coordination overhead (AI handles information routing)
- Eliminate tool friction (integrate systems, don't just connect them)
- Optimize for human-AI collaboration (right tasks to right actors)
- Build in verification (catch errors early, not late)
- Enable continuous improvement (capture learnings as work happens)

**Phase 4: Implementation Roadmap & Economic Model (Week 5-6)**

You get:
- Detailed implementation plan with sequencing and dependencies
- Change management approach (how to bring teams along)
- Economic model with ROI projections over 12-24 months
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategies
- Pilot design if you want to start small

---
