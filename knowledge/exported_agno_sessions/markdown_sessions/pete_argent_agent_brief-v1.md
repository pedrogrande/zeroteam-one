# Pete Argent — agent brief: vision, mission, values, strengths & growth edges

**Prepared for:** Agent / collaborator reference  
**Prepared by:** Pete Argent  
**Date:** April 2026

---

## Who Pete is

Pete Argent is a Melbourne-based social innovator, educator, and entrepreneur with over 20 years of experience at the intersection of technology, education, and human-centred design. He has built and led coding schools, delivered digital skills training in collaboration with the United Nations (ITU) across India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Iran, and the Philippines, lectured in blockchain economics at RMIT University, and founded multiple education ventures. His career reflects a consistent throughline: using technology as a vehicle for human empowerment, particularly for young people in underserved communities.

Pete is the founder of **Zero Team Accelerator** — a global, youth-led movement designed to equip young people with Web3 digital skills, decentralised governance knowledge, and the tools to create real-world impact through technology.

---

## Vision

Pete's vision is bold and long-horizon: a **post-scarcity global economy** where every person — regardless of geography or background — has access to the knowledge, skills, and opportunities needed to live a meaningful, productive, and prosperous life.

More specifically, he envisions:

- A **global network of motivated young people** collaborating across borders to build technology solutions to the problems in their communities
- A world where **Web3 digital skills** — computer science fundamentals, design thinking, blockchain economics, AI, ethical governance, and circular economy design — are taught to school-age children as standard
- **DAOs (Decentralised Autonomous Organisations)** as the dominant model for how humans coordinate production, reducing cost, increasing trust, and distributing value more equitably
- **Learning as a lifelong, enjoyable, purposeful pursuit** — not a painful obligation of an outdated system
- Technology that is human-centred, ethical, and open — not concentrated in the hands of a privileged few

> *"My vision is to build a global network of motivated young people, working together, sharing their ideas and knowledge, collaborating to build technology solutions to the problems they face in their communities, speeding up human advancement, eliminating suffering, and bringing prosperity to everyone."*  
> — Pete's Manifesto

## Mission

Pete's five-year mission (to 2030) is to **empower youth worldwide to develop their strengths, acquire digital skills, and lead ethical, decentralised innovations** that shape a more inclusive, equitable, and opportunity-rich future.

In practice this means:

- **Bridging the gap between education and opportunity** — providing accessible, skill-based, real-world learning experiences that existing systems have failed to deliver
- **Democratising innovation and governance** — through decentralised, trust-based collaboration models (DAO structures)
- **Fostering economic empowerment** — enabling youth to earn through ethical incentives, digital entrepreneurship, and impact-driven consulting
- **Cultivating future-ready leadership** — training members in emerging technologies, governance, and cross-disciplinary problem-solving
- **Strengthening global collaboration** — uniting youth across cultures to tackle local and global challenges using technology and innovation

Zero Team Accelerator will achieve this by building a decentralised digital platform — a hub for learning, missions, governance, collaboration, and self-development — with pilot regions in India, Sri Lanka, and Australia before scaling globally.

## Strengths

Pete completed the Gallup CliftonStrengths assessment in 2018. His top five signature themes are:

### 1. Futuristic

Pete is energised by visions of what *could* be. He naturally sees possibilities over the horizon and uses those pictures to pull himself — and others — forward. This is the engine behind Zero Team Accelerator: a clear, detailed, inspiring picture of a post-scarcity world powered by decentralised technology and empowered youth. He is most effective when given the space to articulate that vision and help others latch onto it.

### 2. Strategic

Pete's mind naturally cuts through complexity to identify the best path forward. He asks *"what if?"* repeatedly, playing out scenarios and discarding paths that lead nowhere. This makes him a strong systems thinker and a clear-eyed strategist when navigating the complexity of building a DAO, designing a learning platform, or working across multiple geographies and stakeholder groups.

### 3. Activator

*"When can we start?"* is Pete's natural posture. He is action-oriented, impatient with over-analysis, and believes that doing is the fastest form of learning. He moves quickly from idea to execution and generates momentum in teams and communities. This is evident in the breadth of initiatives he has launched — coding schools, ITU training programs, university courses, and now Zero Team Accelerator.

### 4. Ideation

Pete is fascinated by ideas — the more novel, the more energising. He finds connections between seemingly unrelated phenomena and delights in turning familiar problems on their heads to find new angles. This strength fuels his curriculum design, his writing, and his platform thinking. He is at his best in environments that reward creative thinking and become frustrated when forced into rigid processes or standard operating procedures.

### 5. Self-Assurance

Pete has deep confidence in his own judgement and perspective. He knows his view of the world is unique, and this gives him the authority to make bold decisions, take on unconventional ideas (teaching blockchain to school children; building a DAO for youth), and resist pressure to conform to mainstream thinking. This is a genuine asset in the innovation space, where courage of conviction matters.

## Growth edges (areas to be aware of)

Pete brings honest self-awareness to this topic. Several patterns emerge from his profile that are worth naming clearly for any collaborator or agent working alongside him:

- **Activator tension with follow-through:** The same Activator strength that generates momentum can mean Pete moves to the next idea before the previous one is fully consolidated. He benefits from collaborators or systems that hold him accountable to finishing what he starts.
- **Ideation overload:** Pete generates ideas prolifically — his own manifesto notes that "if I don't capture it straight away, I'll forget it." The risk is idea overwhelm: too many directions, not enough ruthless prioritisation. He works best with partners who can help funnel ideation into focused execution.
- **Self-Assurance and feedback:** High self-assurance is a genuine asset but can occasionally create a blind spot to external input or dissenting perspectives, particularly when Pete has strong conviction about a path. Effective collaborators are those who engage him with evidence and curiosity, not deference.
- **Complexity appetite vs. communication clarity:** Pete thinks naturally in complex systems — DAOs, cryptoeconomics, post-scarcity economics — but acknowledges his own manifesto can "sometimes jump between topics." For agent and stakeholder communications, simplification and narrative focus are important.
- **Bootstrapped resource context:** Pete operates as a solo founder at the pre-revenue stage of Zero Team Accelerator. Execution capacity is currently limited by the absence of a full team. He is actively seeking collaborators, co-founders, and support structures.

## Working with Pete — what to expect

- **High-energy, ideas-first conversations.** Expect big picture thinking, rapid ideation, and genuine passion for the mission. Ground conversations in the practical when needed.
- **Collaborative by design.** Pete believes deeply in collective intelligence and will genuinely seek your perspective. He values partners who bring complementary strengths to areas where he is thinner.
- **Values alignment matters.** Pete does not separate his work from his beliefs. Partners and agents who share a commitment to equity, open knowledge, and ethical technology will find him deeply committed and generous. Misalignment on these is a genuine friction point.
- **Flexibility with accountability.** Pete works flexibly and asynchronously — he's across multiple time zones and projects. He responds well to clear commitments and mutual accountability frameworks, and less well to over-prescribed processes.
- **Long-game orientation.** This is not a short-term project. Pete is building something he believes will take 5–10 years to realise its full potential. Agents and collaborators who can think in longer horizons will serve him best.
