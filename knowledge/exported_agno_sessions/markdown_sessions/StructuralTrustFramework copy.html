Structural Ethics

An actor without required capability → cannot perform action

An output without verified evidence → cannot be accepted

A decision without independent review → cannot proceed

The system is trustworthy because of how it was built, not what its builders intended.

Trust Principles (Sample)

T1: Verification Precedes Trust

No output accepted on assertion. Independent verification required.

T2: Proof Is the Product

Structured evidence that work meets specification, not just the work itself.

T4: All Actions Witnessed

Every action recorded in immutable audit log.

T5: No Self-Judgment

Executor cannot be verifier. Structural independence enforced.
