# Ideal Persona Profile Template 

## Attract Amazing Customers

This template guides you through an engaging and thought-provoking activity to envision your ideal customer and then aim to understand and empathize with them. By intentionally planning to attract ideal customers, you increase your chances of finding them \- so it's crucial to have a clear idea of who they are.

---

# Section 1: Questions to Uncover the Ideal Customer Profile

**Instructions:**

1. **Imagine your "best-case scenario" customer.** They do and say everything you'd hope they do, to make your business successful and enjoyable.  
2. **Reflect on past experiences.** Have you worked with a customer who was truly amazing?  
3. **Consider your personal network.** Is there someone in your life who would be a great customer?

| Questions | Your Answers |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Ideal Actions**  What did your favorite customer do or say that made them so great? (If a new business, imagine what they would say or do) |  |
| **Organisation Growth** To really help your organization grow, be successful, and achieve its goals, what characteristics would your customer/s need to have? (What is their personality?) |  |
| **Personal Situation** Are there any details about their personal situation that makes them such a great customer?  Do they know certain types of people? Do they have influence? |  |
| **First interactions** When your ideal customer first interacts with your organization, what is their mindset or approach? |  |
| **Product & Services Interactions** When your ideal customer is actively receiving and then using/benefiting from your products and services, are they doing or saying anything that helps your organization? |  |
| **Continuous Improvements** To enable the continuous improvement of your products and services, what would ideal customers say or do? |  |
| **Top Characteristics** What are 10 characteristics of their personality that make them the ideal customer? (Or perhaps you can rank a list of personality characteristics) |  |
| **Top Values** What are 5 values your customer has that align with your organization? |  |

| Looking at the Converse: It can be helpful to also imagine awful customers and what you don’t want to Imagine the worst customer you've dealt with (or can imagine): What did this customer do or say (or not do or say) that made the experience so unpleasant and of negative value to the organization? What might be a few characteristics of their personality or values? |

# Section 2: Empathy Map Questions

| Questions | Your Answers |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Name**:  What is my name? |  |
| **Tagline**:  Brief sentence that describes me? |  |
| **Bio**:  What should you know about me? |  |
| **Goals & motivations:**  What drives me?  What do I want to achieve? What am I trying to get done? |  |
| **Ideal experience:**  What is my ideal experience?  What do I feel?  What makes it memorable?  What makes it innovative? |  |
| **My Needs:**  What do I really need from this?  What do I definitely not want? |  |
| **Frustrations**:  What frustrates me? |  |
| **Says**:  What do I say about this when talking to friends, colleagues, family? |  |
| **Thinks**:  What are my thoughts about this? |  |
| **Feels**:  How do I feel about this? |  |
| **Behaviors**:  What do I normally do? |  |
