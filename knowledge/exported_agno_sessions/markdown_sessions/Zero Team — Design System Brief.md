# Zero Team — Design System Brief

## Use this prompt with Claude Design or a designer to develop a cohesive visual identity system.

---

## The Prompt

**Zero Team**, an Australian social enterprise that builds trust between people, organisations, and AI. I need a comprehensive visual identity system — not just a logo, but the full design language: colour palette, typography, spacing, iconography, component patterns, and application examples.

### Brand Context

**What Zero Team does:** Practitioner-led AI capability and governance consulting. We train workforces, build structural governance architecture, and implement trustworthy AI systems — all in the same engagement. We serve mission-aligned organisations (NFPs, mutuals, healthcare, education, professional services, B-Corps) and individual practitioners.

**Brand archetype:** Primary: The Sage (deep knowledge shared generously). Secondary: The Caregiver (genuinely invested in client outcomes, not as performance but as design principle).

**Brand personality:** The most knowledgeable person in any room, and the least likely to make you feel it. Direct without being blunt. Warm without being soft. Quietly confident. Australian in disposition — unpretentious, plain-talking, anti-BS. Generous with what they know.

**One-word descriptor:** Grounded.

**Core values:**
1. Trust is the architecture, not the aspiration
2. Capability over dependency
3. Generosity as a design principle
4. Inclusive by design
5. Profitable impact

**Key brand line:** "The value you get from AI depends on the trust you build around it."

**Tone of voice:** Plain language for complex ideas. Active voice, short sentences. Questions more than declarations. Occasional dry humour. Australian cadence — direct, unpretentious, relaxed under pressure.

### The Name — Zero Team

The name draws from two sources:
- **Asimov's Zeroth Law** — "A robot may not harm humanity, or through inaction allow humanity to come to harm." The law above all laws. The principle that no other rule can override.
- **Team** — partnership over authority, belonging over transaction, the human dimension the AI industry forgets.

Together: the team that puts human flourishing first, before everything else, by design.

The "0" in Zero is a potential logo asset — it represents the zeroth principle, a starting point, the circular nature of trust. Consider how the zero/circle can function as a brand mark.

### Visual Identity Direction

**Overall aesthetic:** Clean, intelligent, warm. Not clinical. Not corporate. Not the purple-gradient AI startup aesthetic. Think: architectural clarity with human warmth. Structural metaphors (blueprints, scaffolding, frameworks) are consistent with the brand's "structural trust" positioning.

**Starting colour palette to explore and refine:**
- **Deep Teal** (#0A5E5C) — trust, intelligence, clarity without coldness. The dominant brand colour.
- **Electric Amber** (#E8930C) — energy, action, the activator colour. Used for accents, CTAs, highlights.
- **Warm White** (#FAF7F2) — background warmth. Not stark white.
- **Cream** (#F0EBE3) — secondary background, section differentiation.
- **Soft Charcoal** (#2D3436) — primary text colour. Warm, not pure black.

These are starting points, not final. Refine them for accessibility (WCAG AA minimum across all combinations), screen rendering, and print reproduction. Explore whether the teal needs to shift warmer or cooler. Consider whether additional tints/shades are needed for UI states, data visualisation, and extended applications.

**Typography direction:**
- Display/headings: A variable serif with personality — warm, intellectual, confident. Fraunces was explored as a candidate (optical size axis, italic personality). Open to alternatives that achieve the same balance of authority and approachability.
- Body/interface: A humanist sans-serif — clean, readable, friendly without being casual. Generous x-height. DM Sans was explored. Open to alternatives.
- The pairing should feel like "a thoughtful academic who also coaches sport" — intellectual rigour with human warmth.

**What to avoid absolutely:**
- Generic robot hands, glowing brain illustrations, circuit board backgrounds, abstract neural network visuals
- Purple gradients on white (the default AI startup look)
- Overused font families (Inter, Roboto, Arial)
- Stock photography of people pointing at screens
- Anything that signals "we are the AI company" rather than "we are the people who help you with AI"
- Dark/techy/futuristic aesthetics — this brand is warm, grounded, and human
- Excessive use of rounded corners and soft shadows (the "friendly SaaS" look)

### What I Need in the Design System

**1. Colour System**
- Primary, secondary, and accent colours with hex/RGB/HSL values
- Extended palette: tints and shades for each primary colour (at least 5 steps)
- Semantic colours: success, warning, error, info states
- Background and surface colours (light and dark mode if applicable)
- Data visualisation palette (5-7 distinct colours that work together)
- Accessibility matrix showing contrast ratios for all text/background combinations
- Colour usage guidelines: ratios, do's and don'ts

**2. Typography System**
- Font families with weight specifications
- Type scale (headings H1-H6, body, small, caption) with sizes, line heights, and letter spacing
- Paragraph spacing and maximum line widths for readability
- Font pairing rationale
- Web font loading strategy recommendations

**3. Spacing and Layout**
- Base spacing unit and scale (e.g., 4px base with multipliers)
- Grid system (columns, gutters, margins for desktop/tablet/mobile)
- Section spacing guidelines
- Component internal padding standards
- Breakpoint definitions

**4. Iconography**
- Icon style direction (line weight, corner radius, fill vs stroke)
- Sample icons for key concepts: trust, governance, education, community, AI, shield/protection, growth
- Recommended icon library or custom approach

**5. Component Patterns**
- Button styles (primary, secondary, ghost, disabled states)
- Card patterns (service cards, stat cards, testimonial cards)
- Navigation pattern
- Form inputs
- Data display components (stat callouts, progress indicators)
- Quote/callout block styling
- Section dividers and visual rhythm elements

**6. Brand Mark / Logo Exploration**
- Explore the "0" as a brand mark — the zeroth principle, the circle of trust
- Wordmark treatment for "zero team" (lowercase per brand voice — unpretentious, approachable)
- Consider: the amber dot within the teal circle as a visual signature (the human element within the trust architecture)
- Logo variations: full wordmark, mark only, horizontal, stacked
- Clear space and minimum size rules

**7. Application Examples**
Show the design system applied to:
- Landing page hero section
- Service/offer card layout
- LinkedIn post template (thought leadership content)
- Presentation slide template (3-4 key slide types)
- Report/document cover page (for Trust Audit, consulting deliverables)
- Email template
- Business card
- Community platform interface concept

**8. Photography and Illustration Direction**
- Photography style guide: what to shoot, how to treat, what to avoid
- Illustration style if applicable (structural/architectural metaphors?)
- Guidelines for data visualisation aesthetics

### Design Principles

These should guide every design decision:

1. **Structural clarity** — the design should feel architecturally considered, like a well-designed building. Every element has a reason.
2. **Warm intelligence** — the design should feel smart but never cold or intimidating. The Sage archetype is generous, not superior.
3. **Grounded confidence** — no flashiness, no trend-chasing. The design should feel like it will age well because it's built on principles, not fashions.
4. **Generous whitespace** — let elements breathe. Trust requires space. Don't crowd.
5. **Inclusive by default** — accessibility is a design constraint, not an afterthought. WCAG AA minimum. Consider cognitive accessibility too: clear hierarchy, predictable patterns, plain language in UI copy.

### Competitive Differentiation

The design should be immediately distinguishable from:
- **Big-Four consulting** (Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, EY) — typically corporate blue, serif-heavy, stock photography, safe. Zero Team should feel more human, more practitioner, more Australian.
- **AI startups** (purple gradients, dark themes, futuristic typography, particle effects). Zero Team should feel grounded and present, not futuristic.
- **EdTech platforms** (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning) — clean but generic, platform-first, learner-as-consumer. Zero Team should feel more like a trusted colleague than a platform.
- **AI influencers** (personal branding, bold colours, high contrast, attention-grabbing). Zero Team should feel considered and quiet rather than loud.

### Brand Admiration References (for tone, not copying)
- **Patagonia** — commercial sustainability anchored in genuine values; design that feels purposeful not precious
- **Stripe** — technical depth communicated with extraordinary clarity; design as information architecture
- **37signals/Basecamp** — opinionated, anti-hype, strong editorial voice; design serves the argument
- **Bench Accounting** — premium expertise made accessible to small organisations; warm professionalism

### Final Note

This is a brand for practitioners, not performers. The design system should feel like it was built by people who care about getting the details right — not because details impress clients, but because structural integrity is the brand's core value. If the design system itself isn't trustworthy, nothing built on it will be.
