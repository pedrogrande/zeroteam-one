# Story Arcs

This is an excellent foundation for a series — and you have significant original IP in your own documents that most Substack writers on this topic simply won't have. Here's a 6-article series plan that builds a compelling, coherent narrative arc from problem to solution.

***

## Series Title & Framing

**"The Trust Gap: Why AI's Economic Promise Is Stalling — And What It Will Take to Unlock It"**

The series follows a classic narrative arc: *establish the stakes → diagnose the problem → deepen the diagnosis → examine the evidence → explore solutions → issue a call to action.* Each article stands alone but rewards readers who follow the whole series.

***

## Article 1: The $15 Trillion Problem Nobody Is Talking About

**Hook:** AI promises to add $15 trillion to the global economy. So why are 74% of the benefits flowing to just 20% of organisations — and why is adoption *flatlined* at 11% of workers for over a year?

**Core argument:** The AI productivity gap is real and growing, but the conventional explanations (regulation, skills, infrastructure) miss the root cause. The missing variable is trust — and nobody in the mainstream AI debate is quantifying what it's actually costing us.

**Key evidence to deploy:**

- PwC's 74%/20% concentration finding [pwc](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/news-room/press-releases/2026/pwc-2026-ai-performance-study.html)
- The Economist / Windfall Trust data on adoption flatlining at 11% [windfalltrust.substack](https://windfalltrust.substack.com/p/brief-3-if-ai-stays-jagged-it-could)
- £29 billion/year lost to AI verification burden in the UK alone [onrec](https://www.onrec.com/news/statistics/ai-mistrust-is-making-work-slower-not-faster-%E2%80%93-uk-employees-spend-almost-as-long)
- $56 billion annual productivity loss from the generational AI trust gap [revenuebrew](https://www.revenuebrew.com/stories/2026/04/16/ai-trust-barrier)
- Van Geelen's "Ghost GDP" framing as the mainstream take you're going beyond [fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/23/will-ai-take-my-job-cause-recession-crash-james-val-geelen-citrini/)

**What you add:** The question Van Geelen raises but doesn't answer — *why* doesn't AI GDP circulate? Because trust hasn't been built at the human level. Frame this as the series' central thesis.

## Article 2: Trust All the Way Down — The Stack Nobody Is Drawing

**Hook:** The AI trust crisis isn't just about whether you trust a chatbot. It's about whether you trust the company that built it, the government overseeing it, and the institutions deploying it. And all three are broken simultaneously.

**Core argument:** Introduce the "trust stack" as your original organising framework — AI trust sits on institutional trust, which sits on social trust. Each layer is degraded. This is why technical fixes alone won't work.

**Key evidence to deploy:**

- Edelman Trust Barometer's long-run institutional trust decline, deployed into AI context [privatebank.jpmorgan](https://privatebank.jpmorgan.com/nam/en/services/portfolio-management/sustainable-investing/insights/how-trust-may-drive-a-competitive-edge-in-the-age-of-ai)

The distinction between *declarative* ethics (we say we'll be responsible) and *structural* ethics (the architecture makes irresponsibility impossible). ## Article 3: Australia's Sceptical Adopter Problem — A Case Study in the Trust Gap

**Hook:** Australia is the world's leading nation of "sceptical adopters" — people using AI every day while remaining fundamentally distrustful of the systems behind it. That paradox is costing us $60 billion.

**Core argument:** Use Australia as a granular, local case study that makes the global trust argument concrete and personal for your audience. Australia's trust deficit isn't just cultural — it's structurally produced by specific, fixable failures.

**Key evidence to deploy:**

- KPMG/University of Melbourne data: 50% use AI, only 36% trust it; 30% believe benefits outweigh risks — lowest globally [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/au/en/media/media-releases/2025/04/global-study-reveals-australia-lags-in-trust-of-ai-despite-growing-use.html)
- Only 24% of Australians have undertaken AI training vs. 39% globally [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/au/en/insights/artificial-intelligence-ai/trust-in-ai-global-insights-2025.html)
- Only 9% have heard of the AI Safety Institute or National AI Plan [minderoo](https://www.minderoo.org/stories/ai-research-feb-2026/)
- 70% with high AI knowledge trust government to use AI responsibly vs. only 12% with no knowledge — your literacy = trust proof point [pmc.gov](https://www.pmc.gov.au/sites/default/files/resource/download/ltbi-ai-surveys-summary.pdf)
- Brooke Wright's "MLM vibes" framing as the cultural diagnosis [ia.acs.org](https://ia.acs.org.au/article/2025/aussies-more-scared-than-excited-about-ai.html)

**What you add:** Connect the literacy gap directly to the trust gap with the government's own data. Argue that a significant chunk of Australia's trust deficit is simply a *communication and education* deficit — solvable before any major policy changes.

## Article 5: What Actually Builds Trust — And What Doesn't

**Hook:** Every organisation now has an AI ethics policy. Almost none have AI trust. There's a reason for that — and it's architectural, not aspirational.

**Core argument:** Distinguish between performative trust-building (ethics statements, responsible AI principles, governance committees) and structural trust-building (explainability by design, human-in-the-loop architecture, co-design with affected communities). Only the second kind works at scale.

**Key evidence to deploy:**

- Your Structural Trust Framework: declarative vs. structural ethics; "principles alone cannot prevent anything" [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_8788bb3e-490d-499d-b897-09db0a68afea/b106ad5d-2c8c-429d-8098-3442af1e217a/structured-trust-framework.md)
- McKinsey 2026: organisations that openly report AI errors build more durable trust than those projecting infallibility [mckinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/tech-forward/state-of-ai-trust-in-2026-shifting-to-the-agentic-era)
- Government transparency statements: mandatory public AI transparency statements now required of APS agencies [digital.gov](https://www.digital.gov.au/policy/ai/list-of-transparency-statements)
- The literacy finding: simply *telling* Australians what the AI Safety Institute does shifts their trust — awareness is itself a trust intervention [minderoo](https://www.minderoo.org/stories/ai-research-feb-2026/)
- JP Morgan's institutional trust-as-moat framing: organisations already trusted by customers extract far more AI value [privatebank.jpmorgan](https://privatebank.jpmorgan.com/nam/en/services/portfolio-management/sustainable-investing/insights/how-trust-may-drive-a-competitive-edge-in-the-age-of-ai)
- Engage with the counter-argument: can trust be *built*, or does it have to be *earned*? (Your answer: structurally enforced trustworthiness earns trust automatically over time)

**What you add:** The Structural Trust Framework's four conformance profiles as a practical ladder organisations can climb. This gives readers a concrete takeaway and positions your framework as a reference point. ## Article 6: The Trust Dividend — What a High-Trust AI Economy Could Look Like

**Hook:** What if we closed the trust gap? Not just for AI companies, but for the whole economy?

**Core argument:** This is your aspirational, forward-looking closer. Model what the economic, social, and democratic benefits would look like if Australia — and the world — moved from the current low-trust AI equilibrium to a high-trust one. Position trust-building as the highest-leverage economic policy intervention available right now.

**Key evidence to deploy:**

- The $60 billion Australia stands to gain by 2035 if the trust gap is closed [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/can-ai-deliver-benefits-financial-services-when-most-dont-o-connor-ruz8c)
- WEF: 78 million net new jobs created — but only if adoption is broad, which requires trust [weforum](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/01/creating-economic-opportunities-for-all-in-the-intelligent-age/)
- Your trust multiplier's compounding effect: early trust-building investments pay increasingly large dividends [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_8788bb3e-490d-499d-b897-09db0a68afea/8399bbc2-28d4-4e6e-bc95-e7eeb6790e93/trust-as-a-multiplier.md)
- The collective intelligence dividend: high-trust AI systems enable knowledge sharing that produces exponentially more value than individual contributions [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_8788bb3e-490d-499d-b897-09db0a68afea/f1f561a2-9a78-432e-a528-1bae4fc44065/collective-intelligence.md)
- Australia's structural advantages: strong research institutions, KPMG/University of Melbourne as a global trust research hub, National AI Plan as a foundation to build on

**What you add:** A concrete "trust dividend" estimate for Australia. A call to action directed at three audiences simultaneously — policymakers (regulate structurally, not declaratively), organisations (adopt the Structural Trust Framework), and individuals (invest in AI literacy as a civic and economic act). End with the series thesis restated: trust isn't the soft layer on top of the AI economy — it *is* the AI economy.
