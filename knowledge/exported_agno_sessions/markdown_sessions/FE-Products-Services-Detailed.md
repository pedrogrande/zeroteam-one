### 1.1 Coordination Cost Snapshot

**What It Is:**
A rapid diagnostic that reveals how much time and money an organization loses to coordination overhead—meetings, status updates, waiting for approvals, context-switching, and tool friction.

**Business Problem It Solves:**
Most organizations don't realize that 40-60% of knowledge workers' time is spent *coordinating* rather than creating value. This hidden tax on productivity costs enterprises millions annually without appearing on any budget line.

**Deliverables:**
- Time allocation analysis (value work vs. coordination vs. tool friction)
- Workflow observation report with task decomposition
- Economic impact calculation (annual cost of coordination overhead)
- Quick-win opportunities (3-5 high-ROI interventions)
- AI readiness assessment for identified bottlenecks

**Client Value:**
- Quantified baseline: "Your team spends 2,400 hours annually in status meetings—that's $240K in fully-loaded costs"
- Clear ROI targets for automation/transformation initiatives
- Data-driven justification for workflow redesign investments
- Identification of processes suitable for AI intervention

**Pricing:** AUD $8,000 - $18,000 depending on team size (1-3 teams)

**Team Structure (Task Marketplace Model):**
- **Discovery Lead** (Pete or mid-career professional): Client relationship, framing, final report sign-off — 8 hours
- **Research Coordinators** (MBA graduates): Interview protocol design, observation facilitation, initial analysis — 16 hours
- **Data Collectors** (Undergrad students): Activity logging, interview transcription, time-tracking analysis, coding — 24 hours
- **AI Execution** (Agents + all members): Interview synthesis, pattern identification, cost calculations, slide production — 12 hours

**Member Learning Outcomes:**
- Undergrads: Ethnographic research methods, task decomposition frameworks, professional interview skills
- MBAs: Consulting engagement management, economic modeling, client deliverable production
- Mid-career: Client relationship management, diagnostic framing, executive communication
- Everyone: Practical experience with AI-augmented research workflows

**Research Value for Future's Edge:**
- Empirical data on coordination overhead across industries
- Validated task decomposition methodology
- Proof-of-concept for distributed team delivery
- Case studies for "coordination tax" economic model

## REVENUE & OPERATIONS MODEL

### Task Marketplace Distribution

Every engagement operates as a miniature task marketplace:

**Revenue Split Example (illustrative):**
- **40%** to task contributors (distributed based on task completion and verification)
- **25%** to engagement lead (client relationship, quality, delivery accountability)
- **20%** to Future's Edge treasury (platform development, overhead, risk buffer)
- **15%** held in quality/revision buffer (released after client acceptance)

**Task Claiming Process:**
1. Engagement scoped and decomposed into tasks with clear outputs
2. Tasks published to member pool with requirements, time estimates, token values
3. Members claim tasks based on capability and interest
4. Completion triggers verification (peer review, client acceptance, or automated)
5. Payment released to contributor wallets
6. Learnings captured and added to pattern library

**Benefits of This Model:**
- Members earn based on contribution, not time
- Transparent economics build trust
- Natural skill development (members gravitate toward challenging tasks)
- Risk distributed (if one member unavailable, task goes to marketplace)
- Quality incentivized (reputation affects future task access)
- Perfect alignment with Future's Edge research mission
