# The Core Idea: Three Agent Layers

The team operates across three layers — **process agents** (who drive each step), **lens agents** (who interrogate outputs through different thinking modes), and **persona agents** (who represent real stakeholders). The human's role is orchestrator, deciding when to summon which agents and when to halt.

***

## Layer 1 — Process Agents

Each agent owns a specific phase of the process and is responsible for producing a concrete output before the next step begins.


| Agent | Phase | Output |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Scout** | Scan real-world examples | A structured brief: what exists, what's working, what's missing |
| **Gap Finder** | Identify framework gaps | A named list of open questions the thought experiment will probe |
| **World Builder** | Set the future frame | A written scenario brief with explicit hypotheses, not predictions |
| **Case Architect** | Create diverse use cases | 2–3 fictional orgs specifically chosen to stress-test the gaps |
| **Deep Diver** | Explore each case | Full internal analysis per org; must include a challenges section |
| **Harvester** | Extract cross-case patterns | A standalone gap report — what did the cases reveal? |
| **Persona Maker** | Define the real person | Empathy map grounded in harvested gaps, not the fictional world |
| **Question Keeper** | Articulate open questions | Separates "strong signals" from "interesting hunches" |
| **Concept Sketcher** | Generate ideas lightly | Frames concepts as experiments, not a product suite |


***

## Layer 2 — Lens Agents (De Bono's Six Hats)

These agents are **summoned on demand** at any step to interrogate the current output from a specific thinking mode. They don't drive the process — they pressure-test it.


| Hat | Thinking Mode | How They Intervene |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 🔵 **Blue** | Process \& facilitation | "Are we in the right step? Should we loop back?" — keeps the process honest  |
| ⚪ **White** | Facts \& data only | "What do we actually *know* vs. what are we assuming?" — challenges invented data  |
| 🔴 **Red** | Emotion \& intuition | "What feels exciting or wrong here, without needing to justify it?" — surfaces gut signals  |
| ⚫ **Black** | Critical \& cautious | "What could go wrong? What are the flaws in this?" — your devil's advocate  |
| 🟡 **Yellow** | Optimistic \& value-focused | "What's the best case? What's genuinely strong here?" — prevents premature pessimism  |
| 🟢 **Green** | Creative \& lateral | "What haven't we thought of? What's the unexpected angle?" — breaks out of convergent thinking  |

A critical design rule: **only one hat is active at a time**, and the 🔵 Blue agent decides when to switch. This prevents the common failure mode of lens agents all talking at once and producing noise.

***

## Layer 3 — Stakeholder Persona Agents

These agents **embody specific real-world roles** and react to outputs from the perspective of someone who would actually live with the outcomes. Unlike lens agents (who apply a thinking mode), persona agents apply **lived context**.


| Persona Agent | Represents | Most useful at |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **The End User** | The person the system ultimately serves | Steps 4–5 (case deep dives) and Step 7 (persona) |
| **The Sceptic** | A non-technical decision-maker who hasn't bought in | Step 2 (gap finding) and Step 9 (concept sketching) |
| **The Operator** | Someone who has to run this day-to-day | Step 5 (challenges section) and Step 8 (open questions) |
| **The Investor / Funder** | Someone evaluating viability and risk | Steps 5 and 9 |
| **The Regulator** | Someone asking about liability, ethics, safety | Steps 2, 5, and 8 |
| **The Future Worker** | Someone whose role is changed by the system | Steps 5 and 7 |

These personas can be **generic archetypes** or **customised to your specific domain**. For your thought experiment, a "Sri Lankan university graduate founder" and an "elderly first-time learner" would each produce very different reactions to the same design output.

***

## How the Human Fits In

Your role isn't to do the thinking — it's to do three specific things that agents cannot:

- **Decide which agents to summon** and when to move between steps
- **Make irreversible calls** — accepting a design direction, killing a concept, choosing which gaps matter most
- **Sense what's missing** — the meta-level discomfort that no agent can feel, like you did when you noticed general-purpose frameworks lacked depth

This maps directly to the HUMAN Framework's C1/C2 principles from your earlier work  — humans at the gates of irreversible decisions, agents doing the heavy parallel lifting in between.

***

## Interaction Model

A practical session flow might look like this:

1. Activate the **Scout** → get real-world brief
2. Summon ⚫ **Black Hat** → critique the brief for blind spots
3. Activate the **Gap Finder** → name the open questions
4. Summon ⚪ **White Hat** → strip assumptions from the gap list
5. Activate the **World Builder** → write the scenario
6. Summon 🟢 **Green Hat** + **Sceptic persona** simultaneously → stress-test the scenario from two angles
7. Continue through steps, calling lens agents and personas as deliberate interrupts — not automatically

The key design principle: **agents don't auto-chain**. Each handoff requires a human "yes, proceed" — keeping the thought experiment genuinely exploratory rather than auto-completing to conclusions.
