# Impacts of Mistrust

Yes, the evidence is substantial and increasingly quantified. Low AI trust doesn't just slow adoption — it actively creates new productivity *costs*.

## The Verification Tax

The most direct productivity impact is the time workers spend second-guessing AI outputs  [onrec](https://www.onrec.com/news/statistics/ai-mistrust-is-making-work-slower-not-faster-%E2%80%93-uk-employees-spend-almost-as-long):

- UK employees in large organisations spend an average of **2 hours 41 minutes per week using AI** — but **2 hours 30 minutes checking, verifying, or redoing** what it produced
- This near 1:1 ratio of use to verification largely cancels out the productivity benefit
- Scaled across the UK's large-business workforce, this verification burden costs an estimated **£29 billion per year** in wasted employee time  [onrec](https://www.onrec.com/news/statistics/ai-mistrust-is-making-work-slower-not-faster-%E2%80%93-uk-employees-spend-almost-as-long)

## Investment Paralysis

Distrust doesn't just affect workers — it freezes capital allocation at the leadership level  [revenuebrew](https://www.revenuebrew.com/stories/2026/04/16/ai-trust-barrier):

- **46% of planned AI investments** are currently paused due to trust concerns, according to a 2026 Gong survey of 2,000 US and UK business leaders
- **75%** of leaders feel their companies are "falling behind" in realising AI's most powerful benefits
- A generational trust gap in AI usage is costing companies an estimated **$56 billion in productivity annually**, with older cohorts disproportionately resistant  [revenuebrew](https://www.revenuebrew.com/stories/2026/04/16/ai-trust-barrier)

## The Deloitte Trust Multiplier

Deloitte's research, tracked via a real-time trust index across 300+ brands and tens of thousands of employees, found a striking asymmetry  [forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/johnwerner/2025/05/07/when-trust-in-ai-leaps-up-productivity-follows/):

- In **low-trust organisations**, introducing AI can produce a **149% decline in trust** — making things actively worse
- Customers who trust a brand are **twice as likely** to engage with its AI tools
- Employees in high-trust organisations show a **2.5x higher AI adoption rate** — directly translating to more productivity gains

## The PwC Concentration Effect

PwC's 2026 AI Performance Study explains why AI benefits remain so concentrated  [pwc](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/news-room/press-releases/2026/pwc-2026-ai-performance-study.html):

- Organisations capturing AI's economic value are **1.7x more likely** to have a Responsible AI framework and **1.5x more likely** to have cross-functional AI governance
- As a result, their employees are **twice as likely to trust AI outputs** — enabling higher usage rates and compounding gains
- This is the mechanism behind the finding that just 20% of organisations capture 74% of AI's economic value  [pwc](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/news-room/press-releases/2026/pwc-2026-ai-performance-study.html)

## Cognitive Costs Beyond Lost Time

Low trust creates psychological friction that erodes productivity in ways harder to measure  [onrec](https://www.onrec.com/news/statistics/ai-mistrust-is-making-work-slower-not-faster-%E2%80%93-uk-employees-spend-almost-as-long):

| Effect | Prevalence |
|---|---|
| "AI burnout" — fatigue from constant output checking | 32% of workers |
| "AI dependence" — routine skills atrophying | 33% |
| "Analysis paralysis" — unable to trust AI or gut | 31% |
| "AI blindness" — losing perspective after repeated prompting | 30% |

## What Drives the Distrust

Workers cite four recurring issues that undermine trust in AI outputs  [onrec](https://www.onrec.com/news/statistics/ai-mistrust-is-making-work-slower-not-faster-%E2%80%93-uk-employees-spend-almost-as-long):

- **Explainability** — 32% can't explain to stakeholders how outputs were generated
- **Security** — 32% don't know where their inputs go or how they're used
- **Consistency** — 31% are unsettled by different answers to the same prompts
- **Accuracy** — 31% encounter factually incorrect outputs; 28% report hallucinations

The emerging picture is that trust isn't a soft prerequisite for AI adoption — it is a **hard economic variable**. Organisations that solve the trust problem don't just feel better about AI; they measurably out-produce those that don't  [forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/johnwerner/2025/05/07/when-trust-in-ai-leaps-up-productivity-follows/).
