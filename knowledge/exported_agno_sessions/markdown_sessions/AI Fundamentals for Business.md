# AI Fundamentals for Business

##### C-AIR102

## Course Description

This course provides important foundational information on what AI is – and isn’t \- for non-technical and non-specialist employees across a wide variety of industries and work contexts. It examines the use of AI in the world generally and in industry specifically; benefits for business of AI tools and technologies; Ethical concerns and other issues associated with AI; and AI adoption strategies and general use-case applications at work.

## Course outcomes

| CLO1 | Apply critical judgement to AI inputs and outputs to assess accuracy, bias, hallucination and appropriateness in professional contexts. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **CLO2** | Identify where AI adds value, where it should be avoided, and how it can be used effectively in workplace contexts. |
| **CLO3** | Assess ethical, legal and governance risks of AI use in relation to data privacy, confidentiality, intellectual property and personal liability. |

## Learner Cohort

This course provides an entry-level overview of the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) tools for staff who are currently digitally literate, however, who have had a limited exposure to the use of AI in their work roles.

This learner cohort will typically have some tertiary education in any given field and be familiar with the general use of information technologies in the workplace (such as email systems, online calendars, word processing and basic spreadsheeting, and the use of browser-based systems to access and process information).
