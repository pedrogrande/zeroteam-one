# What Zero Team Looks Like When It's Working

## The Engagement Spectrum

Zero Team would likely operate across four distinct engagement types, each at a different depth and price point: 

***

### 1. 🔍 Discovery Workshops
*"Get clear before you commit"*

A half-day or full-day session with a leadership team — the entry point for most organisations. The goal isn't to deliver answers, it's to surface the right questions: What AI is already happening in your institution? Where are the unmanaged risks? What does your community expect of you? What's your values position?

**What this looks like in practice:**
- Pete or Gagan facilitating with a school's executive leadership team, a college's senior staff, or an RTO's CEO and CLO
- Structured activities — not lecture — mapping current AI use, risk exposure, and readiness gaps
- Output: a prioritised AI readiness audit and a co-designed roadmap for what comes next
- Duration: half-day to full-day; could run on-site at CEM's system office, Wesley's St Kilda Road campus, or remotely for Open Colleges' distributed team 

**Why it works:** Low commitment for the client, high trust-building for Zero Team. Alex gets immediate value — a defensible map of their situation — without signing up for a long programme. 

***

### 2. 📐 Governance Framework Co-Design
*"Build something your institution owns"*

A multi-session engagement — typically 4–8 weeks — where Zero Team works alongside leadership to build a bespoke AI governance framework. Not a template handed over, but a co-designed document that reflects the institution's values, regulatory obligations, and operational reality. 

**What this looks like in practice:**
- For **CEM**: a cascading system-wide AI policy framework — covering acceptable use, student data, child safety, academic integrity, and the Catholic social tradition's perspective on technology — designed to flow from the system office to 300 principals 
- For **Wesley**: a three-campus AI governance structure with differentiated guidance for heads of campus, teachers, and support staff, plus a parent communication framework 
- For **Open Colleges**: an AI risk register mapped to the Standards for RTOs 2025, with governance procedures for AI-generated content and learner support agents 
- Deliverables: a governance document, an implementation guide, a communication toolkit, and a 90-day activation plan

**Why it works:** This is the "programme of work, not a one-off engagement" that the doc identifies as Zero Team's highest-leverage model. It creates genuine institutional capability — not just a PDF — and positions Zero Team as a long-term partner. 

***

### 3. 🧑‍🏫 Professional Learning Series
*"Build the people, not just the policy"*

A structured multi-session learning programme for educators, trainers, or institutional staff — delivered in person, online, or hybrid. This is where Zero Team's educator DNA is most visible and most credible. 

**What this looks like in practice:**
- **For teachers (CEM / Wesley):** A 4-session AI literacy series — responsible AI in the classroom, AI-assisted lesson planning and feedback, academic integrity in a generative AI world, and talking to students and parents about AI. Instructor-led, not self-paced. Pete in the room (or on screen), drawing on 20+ years of classroom experience 
- **For trainers and lecturers (Open Colleges / Kaplan):** A series covering responsible AI use in VET delivery, AI-assisted feedback and marking, ASQA/TEQSA compliance implications, and assessment redesign for academic integrity — built specifically for the regulatory context each cohort lives in 
- **For licensed professionals (Kaplan):** An AI literacy module embedded in financial planning, mortgage broking, or real estate programmes — preparing graduates for responsible AI use in their licensed profession, co-branded and delivered at scale 
- **For principals and institutional leaders:** A separate, shorter leadership-specific series covering strategic AI governance, staff management in an AI context, parent and community communication, and how to engage with regulators 

**Why it works:** Professional learning is recurring, relationship-deepening, and scalable. Once Zero Team has designed the series for one organisation, the modular architecture means it can be adapted rapidly for the next.

***

### 4. 🤖 Agentic System Design & Deployment
*"Build the tools your institution actually needs"*

For organisations ready to go beyond training into deployment — designing and implementing responsible AI agents tailored to their specific operational context. This is Gagan's domain, and it's what separates Zero Team from every other education-sector AI training provider. 

**What this looks like in practice:**
- **For Open Colleges:** A learner support triage agent — handling enrolment queries, extension requests, and course navigation questions, with transparent escalation to human trainers. Built with ASQA compliance requirements embedded, not bolted on 
- **For Acknowledge Education:** A multilingual student support and navigation agent for international students — available outside business hours, with clear human escalation pathways and TEQSA-compliant data handling 
- **For Wesley:** A marking and feedback copilot — generating first-draft feedback on student work that teachers review, refine, and personalise before returning to students 
- Each deployment includes a **trust architecture review** — audit trails, oversight mechanisms, human-in-the-loop checkpoints, and a governance wrapper — so Alex can point to the system and say "this is how we manage it" 

**Why it works:** This is the highest-value, highest-margin engagement type — and it creates deep lock-in because Zero Team becomes infrastructure, not just a consultant. It also generates the most compelling case study material for every subsequent sales conversation.
