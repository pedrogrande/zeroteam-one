Yes, there is strong and recent data to support this — and some of the most relevant research comes from right here in Melbourne.

## The Global Trust Gap

The most comprehensive evidence comes from the **University of Melbourne / KPMG global study** (2025), which surveyed 48,340 people across 47 countries  [mbs](https://mbs.edu/faculty-and-research/trust-and-ai):

- Only **46% of people globally** are willing to trust AI systems  [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/ai-and-technology/trust-attitudes-and-use-of-ai.html)
- **83%** believe AI will deliver wide-ranging benefits — yet trust still trails well behind awareness and usage  [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/ai-and-technology/trust-attitudes-and-use-of-ai.html)
- **70%** believe national and international regulation is needed before they'll trust AI more  [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/ai-and-technology/trust-attitudes-and-use-of-ai.html)
- Alarmingly, **66% of users rely on AI output without evaluating its accuracy**, and **56% report making mistakes** in their work because of AI — undermining the productivity case  [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/ai-and-technology/trust-attitudes-and-use-of-ai.html)

## Australia Lags Even Further

Australia's trust deficit is notably worse than the global average  [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/au/en/media/media-releases/2025/04/global-study-reveals-australia-lags-in-trust-of-ai-despite-growing-use.html):

- **50%** of Australians use AI regularly, but only **36%** are willing to trust it
- **78%** of Australians are concerned about negative outcomes from AI
- Despite Australian financial institutions achieving **67% AI adoption** (above the 57% global average), only **30% of Australians** believe AI's benefits outweigh its risks  [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/can-ai-deliver-benefits-financial-services-when-most-dont-o-connor-ruz8c)
- This adoption-trust gap directly threatens the projected **$60 billion GDP contribution** AI could make to Australia by 2035  [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/can-ai-deliver-benefits-financial-services-when-most-dont-o-connor-ruz8c)

## Enterprise Scaling Is Stalled

The trust problem isn't just public sentiment — it manifests inside organisations too  [cdomagazine](https://www.cdomagazine.tech/aiml/the-state-of-ai-reliability-why-trust-is-becoming-the-biggest-barrier-to-scaling-ai):

- Only **18% of organisations** have scaled AI across multiple teams
- **61%** report experiencing "silent failures" in their data pipelines
- Only **29%** have a clear definition of what AI readiness even means
- As one CDO Magazine report put it: *"Organisations aren't struggling to adopt AI; they are struggling to trust it at scale"*  [cdomagazine](https://www.cdomagazine.tech/aiml/the-state-of-ai-reliability-why-trust-is-becoming-the-biggest-barrier-to-scaling-ai)

## Data Trust: The Hidden Blocker

A separate layer of trust — around data quality and governance — also stalls adoption  [newicon](https://newicon.net/ai-adoption):

- Organisations want AI's benefits but fear data mismanagement exposure
- Key issues include siloed data, unclear ownership, inconsistent quality, and outdated governance models
- The core finding: **when people don't trust the data, they don't trust the outputs — and when they don't trust outputs, they don't experiment**, so innovation stalls before it begins  [newicon](https://newicon.net/ai-adoption)

## Who Trusts AI Least

Trust is not evenly distributed across society, which compounds the equity challenge  [natcen.ac](https://natcen.ac.uk/publications/how-does-public-feel-about-artificial-intelligence-ai):

- People from **lower socioeconomic backgrounds** are less likely to trust AI and more likely to feel it reinforces existing inequalities
- Concern about AI in high-stakes decisions (welfare assessments, loan approvals, policing) has **risen sharply since 2022/23**
- In the UK, concern about facial recognition in policing is significantly higher among Black (57%) and Asian (52%) adults versus the general population (39%)  [natcen.ac](https://natcen.ac.uk/publications/how-does-public-feel-about-artificial-intelligence-ai)

The data is clear: the trust deficit is not a peripheral issue — it is a **central bottleneck** between AI's theoretical economic potential and its real-world realisation.
