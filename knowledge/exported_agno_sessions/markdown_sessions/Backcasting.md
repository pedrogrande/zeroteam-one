# Zero Team Backcasting: From May 2027 to Today

*Backcasting asks: given that vision is real in May 2027, what must have been true at each prior point for it to be reachable? The constraint that makes this exercise honest is this: 30 roles, filled with undergraduate and MBA students, capped at 5 hrs/week during semester and 10 hrs/week during university breaks.*

***

## The Hard Arithmetic First

Before any milestone planning, the capacity reality must be named plainly:

- **Melbourne university semesters:** Semester 1 runs roughly March–June; Semester 2 runs July–November; breaks fall in late June/early July (~3 weeks) and November–February (~14 weeks)[^1]
- **30 students × 5 hrs/wk** during ~38 semester weeks = **5,700 student-hours** across the year in semester
- **30 students × 10 hrs/wk** during ~14 break weeks = **4,200 student-hours** in breaks
- **Total annual capacity: ~9,900 student-hours** — roughly 5 full-time equivalents across the whole year
- The Christmas/New Year window (late November 2026 to mid-February 2027) is the **single largest capacity window** — 10 hrs/wk from 30 students = ~3,600 hours in 12 weeks. This is where the heaviest delivery work must land

The vision is achievable, but only if every role is designed around *leverage* — student effort that compounds, rather than effort that resets each week. Agentic workflows become the architecture that makes this possible: students configure systems that continue to produce output even when they're offline studying for exams.[^1]

***

## The Non-Negotiable Constraint: Roles Must Be Re-Scoped

The 30 roles in the vision are not 30 full-time jobs — they are **30 domains of ownership**, each executed at 5–10 hrs/week through AI-amplified workflows. The students who succeed are those who quickly learn to build agentic systems that do the *execution* while they do the *thinking and directing*. This is itself a live demonstration of the Structural Trust Framework's core thesis: trustworthy outcomes don't require proportional human effort — they require the right architecture.[^2]

The roles divide into three real capacity tiers:


| Tier | Examples | Why They Work at 5–10 hrs/wk |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Agent-amplified output roles** | Content Strategist, Research Analyst, Growth \& SEO, Data Analyst | A well-configured agentic workflow can draft, schedule, and publish; student owns curation and quality gate |
| **High-leverage coordination roles** | Chief of Staff, Head of Community, Events Coordinator, Student Programs Coordinator | Most value comes from structure and relationships; agentic tools handle tracking and comms |
| **Deep-skill roles that need breaks** | Senior AI Governance Consultants, Agentic AI Architect, Full-Stack Engineers, Blockchain Engineer | Require focused sprint work — viable only during semester breaks when capacity doubles [^1] |

*What must be in place for the final sprint to close at target.*

- The **Content Strategist** is on autopilot — the 12-month calendar is executing via scheduled agentic workflows; the student's weekly hours go to editing and engagement, not production

**Backcasting checkpoint:** By February 2027, all core agentic systems must already be live and running well enough that semester constraints don't collapse output.

### ◀ November 2026–February 2027 — The Make-or-Break Window (10 hrs/wk)

*This is the most critical period in the whole 12 months. It is the longest, highest-capacity stretch of the year and it falls exactly halfway through.*

At 30 students × 10 hrs/wk × 12 weeks = **3,600 hours** — roughly one full-time year of work in a single break window.[^1]

What must be achieved here:

- **Product Engineering sprint:** The full-stack engineers and AI/Agentic Systems Engineer deliver the public beta of the workflow design tool. This is the single most technically complex deliverable in the whole vision and it can only happen with sustained focused hours[^2]
- **Blockchain layer implementation:** The Evidence Gate and smart contract layer for the credentialling system require deep, sequential work — this is the window[^3]
- **Workshop portfolio productisation:** The LX Designer and AI Literacy Facilitator run their highest volume of sessions (summer corporate training budgets are live), building toward the 54-session annual target
- **STF implementation engine:** The governance consultants run engagements 5–9 of 13, generating the case study library that becomes the most powerful sales asset in H1 2027
- **Research Lab publishing sprint:** Fellows draft their working papers and working paper circulates for pre-review

**Backcasting checkpoint:** By November 2026, all platform architecture decisions must be made, the product roadmap locked, and the engineering team fully onboarded — so that the break window is execution, not setup.

### ◀ August–October 2026 — Semester 2 Delivery (5 hrs/wk)

*The system is live and the first proof assets must be accumulating.*

This is the hardest semester operationally — students are back at full study load, but the commercial engine needs to demonstrate early traction to convert inbound interest from the Substack launch.

- The **Head of Commercial** has the pricing architecture live and is working the pipeline. First STF engagements (1–4) are contracted and being delivered; at 5 hrs/wk, each consultant runs one engagement at a time, leaning heavily on their agentic workflow templates[^2]
- The **PR \& Media Relations Manager** achieves the October 2026 third-party media citation milestone — this requires relationship work through winter and pitching in September/October; entirely viable at 5 hrs/wk if the student is disciplined about one or two key journalist relationships[^4]
- The **Content Strategist** and **Research Analyst** are publishing on the Substack calendar; agentic drafting tools mean the student's 5 hrs/wk goes to insight, not production
- **Vibe Club, Good Vibes Only, ESA workshops** are running — the Events Coordinator is using the break-period preparations and can execute events with 5 hrs/wk of oversight because the runbooks are already built
- The **Growth \& SEO Lead** has the site and Substack optimised; most SEO work is front-loaded and compounds passively[^4]

**Backcasting checkpoint:** By August 2026, the first 2–3 workshop sessions must have run, the Substack must be live with at least 3 published articles, and at least 1 STF engagement must be contracted.

### ◀ Late June–Late July 2026 — Semester 1 Break: The Launch Sprint (10 hrs/wk)

*Three weeks. The highest-intensity, most critical setup window in the program. Everything that follows depends on what is built here.*

At 30 students × 10 hrs/wk × 3 weeks = **900 hours** — modest in absolute terms but timed perfectly as the transition from setup to execution.[^1]

This window must produce:

- **All agentic workflow systems built and tested:** Every role that depends on agent amplification must have their core system live before Semester 2 begins. A student who enters Semester 2 without their agentic toolkit is immediately overwhelmed at 5 hrs/wk[^1]
- **The Substack series live:** Articles 1–2 of the Trust Gap series published; the content calendar automated through to December
- **First workshop delivered:** The LX Designer and Facilitator run the first productised session — proof that the format works without Pete in the room
- **Commercial pipeline seeded:** The Head of Commercial has initial outreach to target enterprise clients completed; 2–3 discovery conversations booked for August
- **Platform architecture decided:** The Head of Product has the technical stack and roadmap locked — not built yet, but designed — so the engineering team can build during the November break
- **Research Lab partnerships initiated:** RMIT and UniMelb contacts engaged; the Research Lab Director has scoping conversations underway

***

### ◀ June 1–21, 2026 — The First Three Weeks: Onboarding and Orientation (10 hrs/wk, still break)

*Students arrive. Most know business. Very few know agentic AI. Everything must be inverted.*

The single most important design decision for the whole program is what happens in the first three weeks. These weeks are still break weeks (10 hrs/wk), but they are onboarding weeks — and the tension between *learning time* and *output time* is at its sharpest here.[^1]

Based on what's already been documented about the student program design, the first three weeks must do five things simultaneously:[^1]

1. **Rapid agentic literacy:** Every student must move from beginner to capable workflow builder in 2–3 weeks. This is the non-negotiable capability unlock — without it, the 5 hr/wk constraint is fatal. Pete's existing evidence shows this is achievable with structured, hands-on learning rather than passive instruction[^1]
2. **Role ownership established:** Each student receives their domain, their 12-month goal (drawn directly from the role descriptions), and the specific North Star metric that defines success for them
3. **Agentic stack chosen:** The team standardises on a shared toolset (Dify, n8n, or equivalent) so that workflows are interoperable and the Agentic AI Architect can support all roles[^2]
4. **Public commitment made:** Each student publishes their role, their goal, and their 12-month commitment on LinkedIn — creating the accountability architecture that sustains output through semester pressure
5. **Pete's leverage model accepted:** Students understand from day one that their job is not to do volume work — it is to build systems that do volume work, and then govern those systems with their 5 hrs/wk

## What Today (May 2026) Must Look Like to Make This Work

*Backcasting lands here. What needs to exist on May 24, 2026 — right now — for June 1 to be viable?*

- **A cohort of 30 students identified and committed:** Not recruited — *confirmed*. The first three weeks are too compressed to lose time to attrition or late joiners
- **The agentic learning curriculum designed:** A 2–3 week structured program that takes a business student with no technical background to capable workflow builder; Pete has already developed the intellectual architecture for this from prior student program design work[^1]
- **Role charters written for all 30 positions:** Each role's 12-month goal, weekly ownership responsibility, key agentic tools they'll use, and the one North Star metric — these should exist before students arrive
- **The shared agentic stack selected:** One toolset, documented, with basic tutorials prepared so no one loses their first week to tool selection paralysis
- **Pete's own leverage architecture decided:** Pete cannot be the bottleneck for 30 students at 5 hrs/wk each. His own role must be clarified — *strategic direction, quality gateway, and external relationships only* — before June 1
- **The first content assets drafted:** At least a draft of *Trust Gap* Article 1 and the first workshop runbook should exist; students inherit these to refine, not create from scratch

The backcasting conclusion is this: **the decisions that determine whether May 2027 is achievable are not made in November 2026 during the engineering sprint, or even in August during Semester 2. They are made in the next seven days**. The cohort, the curriculum, the role charters, and the tool stack — if these are not resolved before June 1, the launch sprint becomes an orientation sprint, and the whole timeline compresses by six weeks that cannot be recovered.[^2]

***

## The Single Biggest Risk

Every bottleneck in this model traces back to one thing: **students who don't build their agentic workflows in the first three weeks will fail at 5 hrs/wk in Semester 2**. The program is not a traditional internship where humans do the work — it is a human-governed AI operation where humans direct systems that do the work. Any student who treats it as the former will hit capacity ceiling in Week 7 of Semester 2 and either deliver poor output or disengage.[^1]

The entire program design, the onboarding experience, and the first three weeks of mentoring must relentlessly reinforce this single principle: **your job is to build the machine, not be the machine.** That principle is, not coincidentally, also the one the Structural Trust Framework was built to prove.[^2]
