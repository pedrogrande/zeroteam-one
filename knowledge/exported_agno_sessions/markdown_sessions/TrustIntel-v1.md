# What already exists

A few related concepts are in circulation — which is actually useful because it signals the idea has legs without having a definitive owner yet:

- **"Trust Quotient (TQ)"** — an existing leadership assessment tool measuring credibility, reliability, intimacy, and self-orientation — essentially "how trustworthy am I?"[^15_4][^15_1]
- **"Trusted Intelligence"** — EY's term for ethical data management. Narrow, corporate, not a movement concept.[^15_2]
- **"Trust Intelligence: A Capacity All Leaders Need"** — a LinkedIn article from November 2025 that introduces the phrase but leaves it largely undeveloped as a framework.[^15_3]
- **WEF's "global operating system of trust"** for AI governance — systemic language, not a human development concept.[^15_5]

None of them own what Future's Edge is actually building — which is something far broader and more human: trust intelligence as a *developable capacity*, at both the individual and systems level, that young people can earn, demonstrate, and teach the world.[^15_6][^15_7]

***

## The whitespace: what Future's Edge can own

Trust Intelligence, as a Future's Edge brand concept, operates at three levels that no existing framework covers together:


| Level | What it means | How FE already embodies it |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Personal TQ** | The ability to be trustworthy, extend trust wisely, and build trust in every relationship | The Trust Score — earned through real behaviour, verified, portable [^15_8] |
| **Systemic TQ** | Designing systems, organisations, and AI that are inherently trustworthy — not relying on goodwill | The DAO's transparent governance, smart contracts, and blockchain-verified records [^15_9][^15_10] |
| **Civic TQ** | Understanding trust as the invisible infrastructure of societies — and being able to build more of it | AI for Good missions, community impact projects, and cross-cultural collaboration [^15_11][^15_7] |

The framing that makes this distinctive: TQ isn't just about *being trusted*. It's about understanding trust well enough to **build it deliberately into everything you touch**.[^15_7][^15_6]

***

## The concept, developed

**Trust Intelligence is the capacity to:**

1. *Understand* how trust works — economically, psychologically, structurally (the insight work we've been doing in this thread)
2. *Earn* trust intentionally — through competence, genuine care, and consistent integrity[^15_7]
3. *Extend* trust wisely — knowing when and how to trust others, even across difference[^15_7]
4. *Design* trust into systems — so trust becomes structural, not just interpersonal[^15_9][^15_6]
5. *Measure* trust's impact — turning an invisible force into something visible and compounding[^15_8][^15_6]

This is learnable. It's demonstrable. And crucially for Future's Edge — it's exactly what the Trust Score already tracks, just named more powerfully.[^15_8][^15_6]

***

## Brand language possibilities

**As a movement identity:**
> "Future's Edge builds Trust Intelligence — in people and in systems."

**As a member value proposition:**
> "Your Trust Score is your TQ. Earn it. Grow it. Take it anywhere."

**As the AI for Good angle:**
> "The world doesn't need more AI. It needs AI with Trust Intelligence."
> *(Directly answers the governance gap your two reports identified — the difference between AI that helps and AI that harms is whether it's been built with Trust Intelligence.)*[^15_11][^15_12]

**As the thought-experiment hook:**
> "IQ measures how you think. EQ measures how you feel. TQ measures how the world responds when you show up."[^15_1][^15_4]

**As the economic thesis, made human:**
> "The most valuable thing you can build isn't a skill or a product. It's Trust Intelligence — because it multiplies the value of everything else."[^15_6]

***

## Why this works as a brand

Three things make it stick:

1. **It's earned, not claimed.** You can't fake a high TQ — it comes from your track record. That aligns perfectly with Future's Edge's verifiable trust score. People are tired of brands *asserting* trustworthiness. Future's Edge *demonstrates* it through a system.[^15_8][^15_7]
2. **It scales from personal to planetary.** A young member in Sri Lanka building their personal TQ, a nonprofit developing trust-intelligent AI governance, a DAO modelling trust-based institutional design — all the same concept, all the same brand.[^15_12][^15_11][^15_6]
3. **It's timely.** In a world flooded with artificial intelligence, positioning Future's Edge as the home of *Trust Intelligence* is a natural, powerful counterweight — not against AI, but as the essential human capacity that determines whether AI serves or harms.[^15_13][^15_5][^15_12]

***
