# are there good types of rules to set for ourselves, or rituals, tools that can help us?

Yes — it really helps to be explicit about rules, rituals, and tools rather than hoping “we’ll just communicate well”. Here’s a menu you and Gagan can pick from and bake into your founder charter.

***

## 1. “Rules of the game” for you both

These are simple, explicit agreements that protect both performance and wellbeing.

- Health-first rule.
    - Either founder can say “I’m at capacity” or call a “yellow flag” day/week; that automatically triggers reprioritisation (not guilt or argument).
    - Link this to your belief that sustainable rituals, not heroics, are what keep founders effective over time.[^1][^2]
- No-surprise rule.
    - No big emotional, financial, or strategic surprises: you each commit to sharing major worries *early* (e.g., before they become a crisis).
    - You also agree not to unilaterally commit the other to major time/energy obligations without discussing it first.[^3][^4][^5]
- Respectful conflict rule.
    - You will disagree, so define “clean conflict”: no personal attacks, no raised voices, no threats to leave in the heat of the moment.
    - Either of you can call a “time-out” if flooded, with a commitment to return to the topic at a named time.[^6]
- Sustainable work rule.
    - Default work hours + protected blocks: e.g., deep work 9–12, meetings 1–4, no slack after 7pm unless pre-agreed crunch period.
    - During crunches, set a time-box (e.g., max 2 weeks), then a recovery period; this mirrors what wellbeing experts recommend about pairing intense effort with deliberate rest.[^2][^7]
- Radical clarity rule.
    - Roles and decision rights are written down (who owns what, who can say “no” to what) and reviewed quarterly.[^4][^5][^3]
    - Ambiguity is treated as a bug to fix, not something to tolerate indefinitely.

***

## 2. Weekly and monthly rituals

Think of these as your “relationship gym”: small, repeated behaviours that keep the partnership strong.[^8][^1][^2]

- 15–20 min daily or twice-weekly standup.
    - Structure: “Top 3 priorities, blockers, energy level 1–10.”
    - This keeps alignment high and gives a low-friction space to say “I’m a 4 today, I’ll move slower.”[^8]
- Weekly 60–90 min Founder Meeting.
    - Agenda:
        - Check-in (how you are, personally, in 2–3 sentences each).
        - Company priorities and decisions.
        - Risks and worries (each names 1–2).
    - Finish with “What did we each do this week that we appreciate in the other?” to keep motivation and gratitude flowing.[^7][^9]
- Monthly Relationship Retro.
    - Questions you can use:
        - “What gave you energy this month? What drained you?”
        - “When did you feel most supported by me? Least supported?”
    - Agree 1–2 small experiments for the next month (e.g., “No meetings before 10am”, “Async decisions written in Notion”).[^6]
- Quarterly Wellbeing Review.
    - Each of you completes a short self-check (see tools below) and shares 1–2 changes you’d like in workload, focus, or support.
    - Treat this as seriously as a strategy offsite; founders who adjust regularly avoid slow-burn resentment and burnout.[^10][^11][^12]

***

## 3. Personal and shared wellbeing rituals

These are the “micro-habits” that keep you each grounded and able to show up well for the other.

- Individual daily rituals (each chooses their own).
    - Examples founders use: brief morning meditation, gratitude list, light exercise, or a no-phone first 30 minutes routine.[^13][^1][^2]
    - The key is consistency; wellbeing writers emphasise that small, repeated rituals have outsized impact over time, even if you don’t notice the effect day-to-day.[^1][^2]
- Start-of-day personal scan.
    - You already track stress via devices; lean into that by making “body/energy check” part of your startup practice.[^14][^1]
    - Each founder asks: “What do I realistically have to give today?” and shares the answer briefly in your standup.
- End-of-week shutdown ritual.
    - 10–20 minutes on Friday: each notes wins, challenges, and one thing you’ll do this weekend to recharge.
    - This creates psychological separation from work and reduces the “always on” mental loop many founders report.[^11][^2]
- Shared “non-work” ritual.
    - Choose something you do together that isn’t about Zero Team: a monthly lunch, walk, or hobby chat.
    - Research and founder stories emphasise that non-work connection strengthens trust and makes hard conversations easier later.[^9][^12][^2]

***

## 4. Simple tools to support all this

You don’t need heavy software — just a few lightweight tools that make reflection, clarity, and communication easy.

- Wellbeing and emotional check-ins.
    - Use a simple recurring form (Google Forms, Notion, Typeform) with 5–10 questions: energy, stress, sense of support, clarity, and workload.[^15][^10]
    - Some teams adapt employee wellbeing survey tools (like Officevibe-style pulse checks) for founders: a short weekly pulse plus space for free-text reflections.[^15][^8]
- “Founder User Manuals” document (living).
    - One shared doc with a page for Pete and a page for Gagan: strengths, drains, stress signs, what helps/doesn’t help, preferred feedback style.
    - This uses your strengths profiles as source material, and you can revisit quarterly to tweak based on experience.[^16][^17]
- Decision log and agreement log.
    - A simple Notion/Docs table where you record major decisions, the reasoning, and who decided.
    - Add a small “wellbeing impact” column (“neutral”, “stretch”, “unsustainable if repeated”) so you see patterns early.
- Conflict “playbook” template.
    - One page that outlines steps when something feels off:
        - Step 1: Name your own feeling and need.
        - Step 2: Request a time to talk.
        - Step 3: Use “I” statements and stick to specific behaviours.
        - Step 4: Agree one experiment and how to review it.
    - This is in line with guidance on structured co-founder conflict resolution that reduces escalation.[^6]
- External support network.
    - Each of you picks at least one external person (therapist, coach, peer founder group) you can talk to when things get heavy.[^12][^11][^1]
    - This reduces pressure on the other founder to be *everything* and is consistent with what many founders cite as key to staying mentally healthy.[^1]

***

## 5. A concrete “rituals \& rules” starter kit for Zero Team

Here is a minimal, realistic set you could decide on in one session and try for 4–6 weeks:

- Rules.
    - Health-first rule with “yellow flag” language.
    - No-surprise rule for big commitments and emotional build-ups.
    - Sustainable work rule: default hours, protected deep work, clear crunch + recovery pattern.
- Weekly rituals.
    - 3×/week 15 min standup (priorities, blockers, energy 1–10).
    - 1×/week 60 min founder meeting (strategy + appreciation).
    - 1×/month relationship retro with written notes.
- Tools.
    - Shared Notion space with:
        - Decision \& agreement log.
        - Founder User Manuals.
        - Retro notes and action items.
    - A simple fortnightly wellbeing pulse survey you both fill in (could be 5 questions in Google Forms).[^10][^15]

Once you have a first draft, you can explicitly reference this “Operating System” in your partnership agreement or an attached Founder Charter, so it feels real and revisitable — not just a nice conversation.
