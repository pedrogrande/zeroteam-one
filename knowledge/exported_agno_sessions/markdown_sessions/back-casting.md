## How This Works

We start at **2030** — your vision fully realised. Then we work backwards, asking "what had to be true?" at each stage.

The discipline is this: **if it didn't have to be true for the future to happen, it doesn't belong in the plan.**

Let me set the canvas. I'll propose what I see. You challenge, refine, correct.

## The Brutal Question

Looking at this chain — Launch → 2026 → 2027 → 2028 → 2030 — I have to ask:

**Is every step truly necessary? Or are we still strategising instead of shipping?**

Let me be more precise. The *minimum* that had to be true to start:

1. **One successful pilot** — you've got people willing to be guinea pigs
2. **Mission Steward keeping you moving** — otherwise you'll strategise past launch
3. **One real project brief** — so learners ship something real
4. **Content for the first rung** — not the whole ladder

Everything else on the launch list? Content for the next rungs. Agent architecture beyond Mission Steward. Multiple partnerships. Financial model fully tested.

**Those are good to have. They're not launch requirements. They're scale requirements masquerading as launch requirements.**

## The Real Backcast — Minimum Viable Launch

Let me try a different version. What actually had to be true to START:

Dimension Minimum to Start
**Pilot** One workshop with 5-10 people. Your friend group. Learn, iterate, document.
**Agent** Mission Steward running. Keeps you accountable to commitments. Tracks what you said you'd do.
**Content** Workshop content. Not the whole ladder. One rung.
**Project Brief** One real brief from one real partner. Learners ship something real.
**Evidence System** Basic documentation of outcomes. What did people learn? What did they ship?
**Frameworks** Published. Version whatever. Open. Invite contribution.

**That's it. That's launch.**

Everything else — multiple cohorts, geographic expansion, governance transition, framework adoption — those come from doing the first thing well, not from planning them perfectly.
