# Bio for Pete Argent

**Pete Argent** is a technology educator, learning experience designer, and digital innovation strategist with over 20 years of experience designing and delivering transformative education programs across Australia and internationally.

Pete's mission is to empower people with the digital skills needed to thrive in an AI-driven economy, and to do so in ways that are engaging, practical, and genuinely fun. He has taught programming, design thinking, product management, blockchain economics, innovation, and entrepreneurship across a wide range of audiences, from school children to university students to corporate and government professionals.

As a lecturer at RMIT University, Pete teaches undergraduate and postgraduate courses in blockchain economics, cryptofinance, and entrepreneurship, earning top student satisfaction scores. Through RMIT Online, he facilitated short courses in UX design, design thinking for innovation, product management, and blockchain strategy.

Pete has collaborated with the United Nations' International Telecommunication Union (ITU) to lead week-long emerging technology and design thinking training programs across six countries, including the Philippines, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, India, Iran, and South Korea. These programs blended cutting-edge technology literacy with real-world problem solving, culminating in hackathon-style pitches addressing the UN's Sustainable Development Goals.

Earlier in his career, Pete founded one of Australia's first coding schools and delivered programs to thousands of learners, from primary school students to career changers to corporates including KPMG and Telstra. He also co-founded Code Club Australia, securing $2M in Telstra Foundation funding to bring coding to hundreds of primary schools nationwide.

Pete brings a distinctive, research-informed philosophy to learning design. He believes learning should be active, confidence-building, and anchored in real-world application. His core approach, which he calls his *LX Truths*, prioritises peer-to-peer learning, inquiry-led facilitation, and the principle that the best learning happens when it's genuinely enjoyable. He is particularly focused on ensuring AI tools *enhance* human thinking rather than replace it, and has developed frameworks specifically designed to preserve learner cognitive agency in AI-assisted environments.

# Pete’s approach to education

Here is a picture of my approach to education, my values, and the innovative ideas I bring to learning experience design.

## Core educational philosophy

Pete's foundational belief is that humanity's advancement is being held back by a lack of digital skills, and that education systems have broadly failed young people by not preparing them for the high-tech world.

He describes his superpower as "inspiring people to learn technology skills and build a fairer future," and sees empowering youth with digital knowledge as his life's purpose. A human-centered, design thinking approach underpins everything he does, from curriculum strategy to community partnerships.

## Core values (his "LX truths")

These are the principles Pete has developed and refined through experience:

* **Student confidence is key** — building confidence directly improves learning outcomes

* **Learning should be fun** — when classes are designed with fun in mind, students learn more and look forward to them

* **You learn more when you teach** — peer-to-peer learning leads to deeper, broader understanding

* **Ask, don't tell** — questions stimulate thinking and encourage students to engage their minds

* **Design all the things** — design thinking is a core skill that should be applied to anything of value

* **Don't fear technology — embrace it** — empowering students to see technology as an opportunity

## What he's passionate about

* Equipping young people, especially in emerging economies, with AI, Web3 and digital skills including coding, design thinking, blockchain economics, and strategic innovation

* Global equity through education: Pete has delivered programs across the Philippines, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, India, Iran, and South Korea via the UN's ITU

* Delivering strategic innovation and profitable impact training for small business owners and the for-purpose sector

* Lifelong learning culture, recognising that as technology displaces traditional roles, people must embrace continuous upskilling

* Building DAOs with young people as a hands-on way to explore new governance and economic models that could lead to a post-scarcity global economy

## Innovation in LX design

* **Peer-to-peer learning at scale** — using students teaching students as a mechanism to scale education without needing to train more trainers, while deepening understanding for both parties

* **Incentivised learning** — going beyond gamification to reward learning in an immutable, blockchain-verified way that is formally recognised

* **Value sharing** — building learning cultures on the assumption that sharing knowledge creates more value for everyone involved

* **Hackathons as learning experiences** — not necessarily coding-focused, but using lean startup tools to pitch and prototype solutions to real-world problems

* **Structured LX design process** — his Learning Experience Design Template takes a deeply considered approach, covering audience discovery, learning theory integration, microlearning (10–20 minute content chunks), accessibility, cultural sensitivity, and long-term impact measurement

* **Mini-hackathons and real-world missions** — program capstones where participants prototype and pitch solutions to UN Sustainable Development Goals

* **Future-focused programs** — concept programs like *Smart City Builders* (AI \+ IoT for urban challenges) and *Global Villagers* (cross-border collaboration) designed to stretch students' imaginations toward real-world impact

## Teaching Style & Approach

Pete employs a **design thinking / human-centered approach combined with a lean startup mindset** when developing curricula, empathising with all stakeholders and iterating toward innovative, outcome-driven solutions. His Clifton Strengths top themes, Futuristic, Strategic, Activator, Ideation, and Self-Assurance, directly reflect how he shows up in the classroom: as someone who sees possibilities, acts on them, and inspires others to do the same.
