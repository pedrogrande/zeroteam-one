P1 — Human Flourishing. The framework exists to serve human wellbeing, not merely technical efficiency.

P2 — Deliberate Complementarity. Humans and agents are partners by deliberate design.

P3 — Structural Ethics. Ethical constraints are built into the system's architecture, not left to policy and good intentions.

T1 — Verification Precedes Trust. No output is accepted on the performer's assertion.

T2 — The Proof Is the Product. The primary artefact of any unit of work is not the output itself, it is structured, attributed evidence that the output meets its specification.

T3 — Authority Is Structural. An actor's authority derives from what tools they hold, not what they claim.

T4 — All Actions Are Witnessed. Every action leaves an immutable, attributed, timestamped record. Nothing is unwitnessed.

T5 — No Actor Judges Their Own Work. The entity that produces an output and the entity that verifies it must be structurally separate — at every scale, for every actor type. 

T6 — Resilience Through Structure. The system produces trustworthy outputs even when individual actors fail, err, or act in bad faith. 

T7 — Verification Is Distributable. Verification does not require a single central authority.

PF1 — Cognitive Diversity By Design. Complex problems require multiple perspectives before solutions are chosen.

PF2 — Validate Before You Scale. No significant work commits to full execution until its core assumptions have been tested through lightweight prototyping.

PF3 — Lifecycle Efficiency, Not Stage Efficiency. Efficiency is measured across the full arc of work — from problem identification to operational delivery — not within any single stage. 

PF4 — Resources Are Proportional to Value. Token usage, computational steps, and energy consumption are design constraints and diagnostic signals.

L1 — Uncertainty Surfaces Immediately. Any actor that encounters a condition it cannot resolve within its authority must surface it immediately, without judgment about whether it is important enough, without social risk, without requiring permission. 

L2 — Irreversibility Demands Presence. Decisions that cannot be undone require explicit human presence and deliberate judgment.

L3 — Knowledge Compounds. Every completed task produces structured learnings that feed into a shared Pattern Library, a living institutional memory of what works, why, and under what conditions.

L4 — Context Is Local, Knowledge Is Global. Each actor receives only the context needed for their specific task, not everything available.

L5 — Problem Precedes Solution. No work begins solution design until the problem it addresses is understood from multiple perspectives, through research, evidence, and empathy with those affected.

L6 — Definition Precedes Execution. No work begins execution until its definition of done is specified, approved, and written by someone other than the eventual performer.
