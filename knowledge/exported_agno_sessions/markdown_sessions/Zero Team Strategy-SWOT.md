# TOWS Analysis

The TOWS matrix converts SWOT into strategic actions by crossing each quadrant.

## SO — Use Strengths to Capture Opportunities

*Aggressive growth strategies — where you're best placed to win*

- **Launch a "Trustworthy AI Governance" consulting package** targeting APS-adjacent organisations before the June 2026 mandatory AI requirement deadline — Pete's Structural Trust Framework IP directly meets this need with no competitor offering it at this  
- **Design a signature public program** ("AI with Confidence" or similar) combining Pete's facilitation excellence with Gagan's live system builds — showcasing the train-and-implement model that competitors can't  
- **Target NFPs with a subsidised cohort model** — leverage social enterprise positioning and Pete's Mission \+ Connector strengths to build sector credibility fast and generate referral  
- **Build a LinkedIn thought leadership engine** — Pete's Writer (unrealised strength) \+ Gagan's Writer (unrealised strength) create a natural content flywheel; the RMIT and UN credibility provides instant authority

## WO — Use Opportunities to Overcome Weaknesses

*Development strategies — where external conditions can compensate for internal gaps*

- **Partner with RMIT or a peak body to co-deliver programs** — this addresses the brand and client pipeline weakness while leveraging existing institutional trust to build credibility faster than cold  
- **Use the June 2026 regulatory deadline as a forcing function** to build and launch quickly — Pete's Activator strength means urgency is energising; external pressure creates a natural "ship now" discipline that mitigates the persistence  
- **Hire or engage a part-time operations/sales person early** — Pete's Organiser weakness and Gagan's Rapport Builder weakness are both real; a third person focused on client relationships and admin is the highest-leverage early  
- **Productise consulting into repeatable packages** — Gagan's Implementer strength can be directed at building scalable delivery systems, reducing the founder time-per-engagement

## ST — Use Strengths to Counter Threats

*Defensive strategies — where your advantages protect against risk*

- **Deepen the structural AI governance IP** to widen the moat against large consulting firms — Deloitte can copy a training course; they cannot easily replicate a practitioner-built, architecturally-grounded framework with Pete's credibility behind

- **Build a case study library early** — client outcomes protect against the reputational risk inherent in a high-trust positioning; each successful engagement is both marketing and  
- **Lock in a small number of anchor clients on retainer** in the first 6 months — reduces revenue volatility risk from economic headwinds and creates a stable foundation during the build
