# Give me a brief on the recommended brand strategy. We've decided on the name "Zero Team"

Here is the Zero Team brand strategy brief, a single reference document the founders can use to brief designers, writers, developers, and future team members.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5]

***

# Zero Team, Brand Strategy Brief

**Document type:** Founding brand strategy reference
**Prepared for:** Pete Argent \& Gagan Dasari
**Date:** May 2026
**Status:** Version 1.0

***

## The Name

**Zero Team** draws from two sources simultaneously, and the tension between them is the brand's entire story.

From Asimov's **Zeroth Law**: *"A robot may not harm humanity, or through inaction allow humanity to come to harm."* The law above all laws. The principle that no other rule can override. In Asimov's universe, it was the breakthrough that made AI genuinely trustworthy, not because it was declared, but because it was built into the architecture.[^4]

From **Team**: the word that grounds everything. Not a firm. Not a platform. Not a consultancy. A team, with you, for you, doing the work alongside you. The word signals partnership over authority, belonging over transaction, and the human dimension that the AI industry consistently forgets.[^2]

Together: **Zero Team** means *the team that puts human flourishing first, before everything else, by design.*

It also works as a sporting metaphor, the team that starts at zero, with first principles, and builds something that lasts. And as a quiet nod to the founders' identity: two people who started from scratch and built something the market needed.

***

## Brand Positioning Statement

> **Zero Team is Australia's practitioner-led AI capability and governance partner for organisations and professionals who want to use AI confidently, safely, and in ways that genuinely work.**

We are not a training platform. We are not a consulting firm. We are the team that builds real AI capability and real governance architecture, together, with clients who care about getting it right.

***

## Brand Archetype

**Primary: The Sage**
We know things deeply and share them generously. We cut through noise with clarity. We are the most trusted voice in the room, not because we claim authority, but because we earn it through rigour, honesty, and genuine expertise.[^1][^2]

**Secondary: The Caregiver**
We are genuinely invested in our clients' success, not as a performance, but as a design principle. Every program, every engagement, every piece of content is built around the question: *what does this person actually need?*[^2]

**Tertiary: The Outlaw**
We refuse to participate in AI hype. We say what others are too polished to say. We publish the governance framework that makes competitors' policy documents look like theatre. We are on the side of the people AI is supposed to serve.[^4]

***

## Mission, Vision \& Values

### Mission

To empower every person and organisation to use AI with confidence, clarity, and conscience, practically, safely, and in ways that genuinely improve work and life.[^2]

### Vision

A world where AI capability is not a privilege of the well-resourced, and where every organisation, from a two-person NFP to a 500-person company, can adopt AI in ways that are trustworthy, human-first, and genuinely impactful.[^6][^4]

### The Three Non-Negotiables

- **Human first, always**, technology serves people; never the reverse
- **Structural not declarative**, we build real capability and real governance, not documents and declarations[^4]
- **Accessible not exclusive**, great AI guidance should reach small businesses and NFPs, not just those with enterprise budgets[^6]

***

## Brand Personality

If Zero Team were a person, they would be:

- The most knowledgeable person in any room, and the least likely to make you feel it
- Direct without being blunt; warm without being soft; funny without trying
- The colleague who gives you their honest opinion when you need it, not the answer you want to hear
- Deeply Australian in disposition, unpretentious, plain-talking, anti-BS, quietly confident
- Energised by ideas and by people in equal measure[^1]


### Five Personality Traits

1. **Grounded**, everything we say is anchored in real practice, not theory
2. **Generous**, we share what we know freely; we don't hoard expertise as a business model
3. **Challenging**, we ask the question others are too polite to ask
4. **Warm**, we build confidence, not anxiety; belonging, not exclusion[^2]
5. **Rigorous**, we hold ourselves and our work to the standard we ask of others[^4]

***

## Tone of Voice

### How Zero Team speaks

- **Plain language for complex ideas**, if we can't explain it simply, we don't understand it well enough yet
- **Active voice, short sentences**, we don't hide behind passive constructions or corporate hedging
- **Questions more than declarations**, we ask rather than tell; we invite thinking rather than deliver conclusions[^2]
- **Occasional dry humour**, purposeful, never forced; used to make a point land, not to seem clever
- **Australian cadence**, direct, unpretentious, relaxed under pressure


### Words Zero Team always uses

*Practical, trustworthy, human, grounded, capable, structural, community, confident, real, together*

### Words Zero Team never uses

*Disruptive, revolutionary, leverage (as a verb), synergy, world-class, cutting-edge, unlock your potential, AI-powered future, seamless, robust (as a lazy adjective)*

### Tone spectrum by context

| Context | Tone |
| :-- | :-- |
| LinkedIn thought leadership | Smart, direct, slightly provocative, the brilliant colleague, not the brand account |
| Program marketing | Warm, specific, outcome-focused, what you'll be able to do, not what you'll learn about |
| Consulting proposals | Professional, precise, human, never templated; always speaks to this client's situation |
| Community platform | Peer-to-peer, encouraging, curious, facilitates conversation, doesn't dominate it |
| NFP communications | Mission-connected, practical, respectful of resource constraints |

## Visual Identity Direction

### Design philosophy

Clean, intelligent, warm. Not clinical. Not corporate. Not the purple-gradient AI aesthetic that every competitor uses. The visual identity should feel like the brand's personality, clear thinking with human warmth.

### Colour direction

- **Primary:** Deep teal, trust, intelligence, clarity without coldness
- **Accent:** Electric amber, energy, action, optimism; the activator colour[^1]
- **Secondary:** Warm white and soft charcoal, grounding, substance, breathing room
- **Avoid:** Cold corporate blues, purple AI gradients, flat startup pastels, anything that looks like it was generated by the same Canva template as every other AI company


### Typography direction

- Modern humanist sans-serif, confident, readable, not aggressive
- Generous whitespace, the brand doesn't crowd or shout
- References: Inter, Neue Haas Grotesk, or similar, clean, contemporary, human


### Imagery direction

- Real people learning, building, and thinking together, not stock-photo faces staring at laptops
- Architecture and structure as visual metaphors, blueprints, scaffolding, frameworks, echoing the Structural Trust Framework's architectural philosophy[^4]
- The human-AI collaboration image: neither replacing the other, both doing something neither could do alone
- **Avoid:** Generic robot hands, glowing brain illustrations, circuit board backgrounds, anything that signals "we are the AI company" rather than "we are the people who help you with AI"


### Logo direction

- The "0" in Zero Team is an asset, the zeroth principle, the starting point, the circular structure of trust
- Consider: a zero that contains something, a team, a person, a framework, rather than sitting empty
- Should work at small scale (favicon, email signature) and large scale (workshop backdrop)
- Must feel equally credible on a consulting proposal for a 500-person organisation and a workshop slide for a 10-person NFP

## The Brand Story

*This is the narrative told in the About page, in pitches, and in Pete's and Gagan's bios.*

Pete spent 20 years watching education systems fail to prepare people for technological change, and then watched the AI training market repeat every mistake at speed. Gagan spent years building AI systems and watching the gap between what governance documents promised and what architectures actually delivered.[^3][^2]

They came together in Melbourne's AI community, a learning experience designer who understood how humans actually build capability, and an AI systems builder who understood how trustworthy systems are actually constructed. They looked at what the market was offering: hype-driven content, policy documents dressed up as governance, and training programs that produced certificates without capability.

And they built something different.

Zero Team is named for the Zeroth Law, Asimov's principle that sits above all other rules, the one that says human wellbeing comes first, by design, not by declaration. That's not just a clever name. It's a design constraint. Everything Zero Team builds, every program, every framework, every consulting engagement, is designed around the question: *does this genuinely serve the people it's meant to serve?*

We're a social enterprise, which means that question applies to how we run the business too.[^6]

## Competitive Positioning

### The positioning sentence

*"Zero Team is where Australian professionals and organisations go when they want AI capability that actually holds up, not another certificate, not another policy document, but genuine, trustworthy, structural capability."*

### The competitive landscape

| Competitor type | What they offer | What they can't offer | Zero Team's counter |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| Big four consulting | Brand, relationships, expensive reports | Practitioner depth; accessible pricing; structural implementation | "We build it, not just describe it" |
| Online platforms (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning) | Scale, accessibility, certificates | Depth, community, governance, human facilitation | "We give you capability, not just awareness" |
| AI influencers / YouTubers | Reach, entertainment, topicality | Accountability, framework, real-world application | "We're responsible for outcomes, not views" |
| Generic AI training companies | Broad topic coverage, availability | The Zeroth Law principle; structural governance; Pete + Gagan's practitioner combination | "We go to the architecture, not just the surface" |

## The Six Brand Promises

These are commitments made implicitly in every interaction, the standard every team member, partner, and piece of content is held to:

1. **We will always tell you the truth**, including when it's uncomfortable and when our answer is "we don't know yet"
2. **We will build your capability, not your dependency**, every engagement ends with you more capable than when we arrived[^2]
3. **We will never sell you what you don't need**, social enterprise values mean our commercial incentives are aligned with your outcomes, not against them[^6]
4. **We will hold our own work to the Zeroth Law standard**, our systems, our data practices, and our delivery are governed by the same framework we ask of clients[^4]
5. **We will always show our working**, our frameworks are open, our reasoning is transparent, our methods are explained not hidden
6. **We will treat every client as a first-class client**, NFPs and small businesses receive the same quality of thinking as mid-size organisations, at a price that reflects their reality

***

## What Zero Team Is Not

As important as what the brand is:

- ❌ Not a technology vendor, we don't resell tools; we help you choose wisely
- ❌ Not a thought leadership performance, we don't publish for engagement metrics; we publish because we have something worth saying
- ❌ Not a big firm in disguise, no offshore delivery, no junior consultants hidden behind senior names, no relationship theatre
- ❌ Not neutral on governance, we have a position: structural trust over declarative ethics, always[^4]
- ❌ Not a charity, social enterprise means sustainable and commercial; we price for value and invest surplus in mission[^6]
