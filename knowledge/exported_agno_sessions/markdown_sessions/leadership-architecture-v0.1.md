# Designing Zero Team Accelerator Leadership Architecture

---

## Design Principles

Before naming roles, let's establish the architecture principles. This isn't a traditional company structure for three reasons:

1. **Your weaknesses are operational execution areas** — so those functions need *autonomous authority*, not just "support"
2. **Your values demand distributed ownership** — centralising power in you contradicts the mission
3. **The model itself is a platform, not a service delivery machine** — it needs stewardship, not management

**The core principle:** You should have *one direct report* — a Chief of Staff/Integrator role who holds the rest of the system accountable *including holding you accountable to your commitments*.

## The Roles (Detailed)

### 1. **Integration Steward** *(Your single direct report)*

**Traditional equivalent:** Chief of Staff / COO hybrid

**Why this role exists:** Your lowest themes are Discipline, Persistence, Personal Responsibility, Planner, and Time Optimiser. You need someone whose *job* is to hold the system together — and that includes holding *you* accountable to your own commitments.

**This person must:**

- Translate your vision into operational reality
- Own the "hard middle" of projects when your energy drifts
- Track every commitment you make and follow up
- Manage your calendar and priorities aggressively
- Slow you down at key decision points (Deliberative check)
- Flag risks you haven't considered
- Run the leadership team meetings and hold each lead accountable

**Strengths needed:**

- High **Deliberative** and **Discipline**
- High **Responsibility** and **Persistence**
- **Harmony** to manage your directness
- Strong **Planner** and **Time Optimiser**

**Critical question in hiring:** "Tell me about a time you held a visionary founder accountable to commitments they wanted to abandon. How did you do it?"

---

### 2. **Flow Steward** *(Operations & Systems)*

**Traditional equivalent:** COO / Head of Operations

**Why "Flow":** Not "operations" — that sounds static. This person designs the systems that let learners, mentors, and partners move through the experience without friction.

**This person owns:**

- The entire learner journey infrastructure
- Technology stack decisions (with your input on open source philosophy)
- Quality assurance and consistency
- Process design and documentation
- Financial operations and sustainability

**Strengths needed:**

- High **Discipline** and **Consistency**
- **Restorative** — enjoys fixing what's broken (your #31)
- **Deliberative** — sees risks before they materialise
- **Arranger** — can manage multiple moving parts

**Open source commitment:** Must be willing to build on open infrastructure, not default to big-tech platforms.

---

### 3. **Community Steward** *(Learners & Alumni)*

**Traditional equivalent:** Head of Community / Student Experience

**Why this matters:** Your model depends on peer-to-peer learning and alumni becoming validators, facilitators, and governance participants. This role builds the *culture*, not just the cohort management.

**This person owns:**

- Learner experience from enrolment to graduation
- Peer-to-peer learning systems (your LX Truth #3)
- Alumni engagement and progression
- Community rituals, norms, and values
- Conflict resolution and trust repair
- Governance participation — preparing alumni to shape the org

**Strengths needed:**

- High **Harmony** and **Empathy**
- **Developer** — builds people
- **Connector** — links people for mutual benefit
- **Responsibility** — follows through on commitments

**Critical skill:** Must understand DAO governance and be able to design pathways for community ownership.

---

### 4. **Ecosystem Weaver** *(Employers & Partners)*

**Traditional equivalent:** Head of Partnerships / Business Development

**Why "Weaver":** Not "sales" — this is about creating a network where value flows in multiple directions. Employers provide briefs; briefs create learning opportunities; learners provide talent; talent creates outcomes; outcomes attract more employers.

**This person owns:**

- Employer partnership development
- Project brief curation and quality
- Sponsor and funder relationships
- University partnership management
- Government and NGO collaborations
- The "employer legibility" of outputs

**Strengths needed:**

- **Relator** — builds deep, trust-based relationships
- **Individualization** — sees what each partner needs
- **Communication** — can translate between worlds
- **Strategic** — sees the mutual value

**Your involvement:** You'll do the vision-selling on strategic partnerships. This person does the cultivation, maintenance, and delivery.

---

### 5. **Proof Architect** *(Portfolio & Credential System)*

**Traditional equivalent:** Head of Product (but for credentials, not software)

**Why this role matters:** The portfolio system IS your product. It's not a feature — it's the core differentiator. Someone needs to obsess over:

- What constitutes valid proof?
- How do we verify it's genuine work?
- How do learners showcase it?
- How do employers interpret it?
- How do we prevent gaming and fraud?
- How does it become portable and learner-owned?

**This person owns:**

- Portfolio architecture and standards
- The trust layer (verification mechanisms)
- Badge/credential design
- Employer feedback integration
- The "portfolio of judgment" methodology
- Open credential standards (W3C Verifiable Credentials, etc.)

**Strengths needed:**

- **Analytical** and **Discipline**
- **Deliberative** — anticipates how systems can be gamed
- **Learning** — stays current on credential innovation
- **Integrity** — high ethical bar

---

### 6. **Content Steward** *(Learning Design & Delivery)*

**Traditional equivalent:** Head of Learning / Academic Director

**Why "Steward":** Content shouldn't be owned by the organisation — it should be stewarded for the community. This role ensures your LX Truths are embedded, content stays current, and quality is consistent.

**This person owns:**

- Curriculum design across the four-layer ladder
- Your LX Truths embedded in every module
- The "anti-lobotomy" AI framework integration
- Facilitator training and quality
- Content currency (AI moves fast)
- Localisation and cultural adaptation

**Your involvement:** You'll co-design the methodology and core frameworks, especially the cognitive preservation approach. This person operationalises it at scale.

**Strengths needed:**

- High **Learner** and **Developer**
- **Discipline** for consistent quality
- **Ideation** — but channelled into curriculum
- **Communication** for teaching facilitators

---

### 7. **Impact Architect** *(Metrics & Evaluation)*

**Traditional equivalent:** Head of Impact / Research & Evaluation

**Why this role exists:** You're a social enterprise. Impact is your product. But your weakness in **Context** (learning from the past) and **Deliberative** means you need someone who can:

- Define what success looks like
- Measure it rigorously
- Learn from what doesn't work
- Report to funders and stakeholders
- Build the evidence base for scaling

**This person owns:**

- Impact metrics framework
- Learner outcome tracking
- Employer satisfaction and placement data
- Longitudinal studies (6 months, 1 year, 3 years)
- The "marketing story" evidence base
- Funder reporting and accountability

**Strengths needed:**

- **Analytical** and **Discipline**
- **Deliberative** — rigor over speed
- **Context** — learns from historical data
- **Responsibility** — for accurate reporting

---

### 8. **Trust Keeper** *(Ethics, Governance & Open Systems)*

**Traditional equivalent:** No direct equivalent — maybe a combination of Legal/Compliance + Ethics

**Why this role exists:** Your values (open source, decentralisation, ethical innovation, trust & transparency) are strategic, not peripheral. Someone needs to be the conscience of the organisation.

**This person owns:**

- Ethical use of AI in programs
- Open source commitments (are we actually walking the talk?)
- Data sovereignty for learners
- Governance design (pathway to community ownership)
- Trust architecture (verification, transparency, accountability)
- Values alignment in partnerships (refusing unethical ones)

**This person must be willing to:**

- Challenge you when you drift from values
- Say no to revenue that compromises ethics
- Design the pathway to decentralisation
- Hold the "open by default" commitment

**Strengths needed:**

- High **Deliberative** and **Responsibility**
- **Context** — understanding precedent and implications
- **Harmony** — but willing to create productive tension
- **Intellection** — deep thinking about ethical questions

## Accountability Mechanisms

Given your weakness profile, the system needs *built-in accountability* to you:

### **Weekly:**

- 30-minute "Commitment Review" with Integration Steward
- What did you say you'd do? Did you do it? If not, why?
- What's at risk? What needs to be dropped?

### **Monthly:**

- Leadership team retrospective (facilitated by Integration Steward)
- What's stalled? What needs persistence?
- Are we living our values?

### **Quarterly:**

- Strategic review — you lead vision, Integration Steward leads reality check
- What's changed? What needs adjustment?

### **The "Stop the Bus" Protocol:**

Any leadership team member can trigger a pause if:

- A decision violates stated values
- You're about to make a unilateral move that affects stakeholders
- A risk hasn't been properly assessed

This isn't bureaucracy — it's Deliberative distributed where you need it.

## Hiring Sequence

You can't hire everyone at once. Recommended sequence:

### **Phase 1: Foundation (First 6 months)**

1. **Integration Steward** — Find this person first. They become your partner in building everything else.

2. **Flow Steward** — Without operational systems, nothing scales. This role builds the infrastructure.

### **Phase 2: Core Delivery (Months 6-12)**

1. **Content Steward** — You can't deliver programs at scale without curriculum consistency.

2. **Community Steward** — Once cohorts are running, community needs dedicated attention.

### **Phase 3: Growth (Months 12-18)**

1. **Ecosystem Weaver** — Partnerships become the growth engine.

2. **Proof Architect** — Portfolios become your differentiation.

### **Phase 4: Maturity (Months 18-24)**

1. **Impact Architect** — Evidence base enables funding and scaling.

2. **Trust Keeper** — As you scale, values drift risk increases.

## Alternative: Lean Launch Team

If funding is tight initially, you could start with three roles:

Role Covers
**Integration Steward** Operations + Accountability + Planning
**Learning & Community Lead** Content + Learner Experience
**Partnership & Impact Lead** Employers + Metrics

Then grow into the full architecture as revenue allows.

## One Provocation

Your instinct might be to hire "people who get it" — visionaries like you.

**Don't.**

You need people who *complement* you, not mirror you. Hire for:

- High **Discipline** where you're low
- High **Persistence** where you drift
- High **Deliberative** where you rush
- High **Harmony** where you're direct

The right Integration Steward will sometimes frustrate you. That's the point. They're the weight that keeps the kite flying.
