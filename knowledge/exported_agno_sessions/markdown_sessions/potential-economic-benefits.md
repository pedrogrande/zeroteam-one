# Economic Benefits of AI

## GDP & Productivity Growth

Forecasts for AI's macro impact span a broad range:

- **Goldman Sachs** estimates AI could boost global GDP by $7 trillion (7%) over 10 years, with US productivity growth rising 1.5% per year  [mitsloan.mit](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/a-new-look-economics-ai)
- **McKinsey** projects AI could add $13–$25.6 trillion in global economic activity, equating to ~1.2% additional GDP growth per year  [nexford](https://www.nexford.edu/insights/how-will-ai-affect-jobs)
- **PwC** puts the figure at up to **$15.7 trillion** added to the global economy by 2030, with $6.6 trillion from productivity gains alone  [aggentic](https://aggentic.ai/blog/economic-potential-ai)
- More conservative estimates from MIT's Daron Acemoglu suggest a **1.1–1.8% GDP increase** over 10 years, arguing only ~5% of tasks will be profitably automated in the near term  [mitsloan.mit](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/a-new-look-economics-ai)
- CBA economists forecast AI lifting productivity by **0.8–1.0 percentage points** in advanced economies over time, including Australia  [commbank.com](https://www.commbank.com.au/articles/newsroom/2026/02/ai-boom-bubble-or-both.html)

## Labour Market Effects

AI's impact on employment is nuanced — it both displaces and creates jobs:

- The World Economic Forum predicts AI will create **170 million new jobs** this decade while displacing 92 million, resulting in a **net gain of 78 million jobs**  [weforum](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/01/creating-economic-opportunities-for-all-in-the-intelligent-age/)
- Firms that use AI extensively show **~6% higher employment growth** and **9.5% more sales growth** over five years  [mitsloan.mit](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-artificial-intelligence-impacts-us-labor-market)
- Job postings requiring new AI-related skills pay **up to 15% more** in the UK and 8.5% more in the US, boosting local consumer spending  [imf](https://www.imf.org/en/blogs/articles/2026/01/14/new-skills-and-ai-are-reshaping-the-future-of-work)

## Business & Sector Productivity

- AI is already lifting real economic activity — in the US, AI-related investment has become a key driver of GDP resilience  [commbank.com](https://www.commbank.com.au/articles/newsroom/2026/02/ai-boom-bubble-or-both.html)
- Companies using AI to automate portions of roles (rather than entire roles) tend to see employment *grow* in those positions, as firm productivity expands faster than headcount is cut  [mitsloan.mit](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-artificial-intelligence-impacts-us-labor-market)
- However, the gains are unevenly distributed: **74% of AI's economic value** is currently captured by just 20% of organisations — those who have moved beyond pilots to full deployment  [pwc](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/news-room/press-releases/2026/pwc-2026-ai-performance-study.html)

## Key Sectors Benefiting

- **Healthcare** — AI accelerates drug discovery, diagnostics, and care delivery
- **Finance & logistics** — automation of high-volume, rules-based tasks  [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_8788bb3e-490d-499d-b897-09db0a68afea/33eeb5c4-0493-4735-a483-268baa07685c/which-sectors-and-industries-m-aoJr_d8UTcq.f8UaBol9hg.md)
- **Software development** — code generation dramatically increases developer output
- **Green energy** — AI optimises grid management and accelerates clean tech R&D  [weforum](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/01/creating-economic-opportunities-for-all-in-the-intelligent-age/)

## The Equity Challenge

The economic upside is real, but not automatic or evenly shared.  [imf](https://www.imf.org/en/blogs/articles/2026/01/14/new-skills-and-ai-are-reshaping-the-future-of-work) Regions with high AI skill demand have seen employment in AI-vulnerable occupations fall **3.6% after five years**, meaning policy choices around reskilling and labour transitions will heavily determine whether the productivity dividend is broadly shared  [imf](https://www.imf.org/en/blogs/articles/2026/01/14/new-skills-and-ai-are-reshaping-the-future-of-work).
