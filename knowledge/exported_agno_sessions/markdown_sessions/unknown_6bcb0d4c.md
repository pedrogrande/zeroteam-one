## 7. THE FREEMIUM BOUNDARY — ANSWERED PRECISELY

### Where Free Ends and Paid Begins

```
FREE                                                          PAID
◄──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────►

KNOW ──── PRACTICE ──── ╳ ──── PROVE ──── SCALE ──── GUARANTEE
                         ▲
                    FREEMIUM LINE
                    ─────────────
                    You can read 
                    everything.    
                    You can try    
                    everything.    
                    You cannot     
                    prove it       
                    without us.    
```

### The Five Tests for the Boundary

**Is it free?** It must pass ALL five tests:
1. Does withholding it create a trust gap? → If yes, it must be free
2. Can a practitioner use it WITHOUT paying for anything else? → If yes, it belongs in the Fractal
3. Does it make the methodology MORE adoptable? → If yes, it's a trust investment
4. Does giving it away contradict the commercial model? → If no, give it away
5. Would Red Hat give this away? → If yes, it's probably free

**Is it paid?** It must pass AT LEAST ONE of these:
1. Does it convert knowledge into demonstrated/provable capability? → Certification
2. Does it make the methodology faster/consistent/reliable at scale? → Tools
3. Does it provide accountability someone can't get on their own? → Enterprise support
4. Does it provide data/comparisons that only exist at scale? → Benchmarks
5. Would you need to hire someone to do this without the product? → If yes, it's worth paying for

### The Four Things That Must Be Free (Non-Negotiable)

| What | Why It Must Be Free | What Would Happen If It Were Paid |
|---|---|---|
| **The complete CAWDP methodology** | Trust requires transparency. A trust company with a hidden process is a contradiction. | Nobody would adopt it. "Trust our methodology (but we won't show it to you)" is absurd. |
| **The complete PRISM ontology** | Data lock-in at the schema layer contradicts openness. | Organizations would fear vendor lock-in. They'd adopt open alternatives (even inferior ones). |
| **The complete 35 principles** | Principles you can't see are principles you can't verify. | "We have 35 principles (trust us)" = "We have 35 marketing claims." |
| **Basic templates for one workflow** | If you can't try it, you won't trust it. The starter pack IS the proof of concept. | Practitioners would give up before starting. The blank page problem kills adoption. |

### The Four Things That Must Be Paid (Revenue Critical)

| What | Why It Must Be Paid | What Would Happen If It Were Free |
|---|---|---|
| **Certification** | Proof requires verification. Self-attestation is worthless. | No revenue engine. No quality signal. No ecosystem of trusted practitioners. |
| **Enterprise tools (Workspace, Schema Library)** | Scale requires infrastructure. Infrastructure costs money. | Can't fund the platform. Can't provide SLAs. Can't invest in enterprise features. |
| **Trust assessments & benchmarks** | Data has compounding value. Every assessment makes the benchmark more valuable. | No data moat. No category authority. No defensible position. |
| **Expert implementation** | Accountability requires skin in the game. Free advice is worth what you pay for it. | No revenue to sustain the company. No one accountable when things break. |

## 9. THE TRUST-REVENUE COHERENCE TEST

Every product must pass this test: **Does selling this product contradict the trust mission?**

| Product | Trust-Renewing Mechanism | Contradiction Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Certification | Certified practitioners are publicly listed and peer-reviewed | Low — certification IS trust | Ensure exam integrity, transparent pass rates |
