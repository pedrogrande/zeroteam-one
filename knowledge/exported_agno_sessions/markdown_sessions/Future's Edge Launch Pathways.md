## Your Strengths Profile: The Honest Read

Before choosing a pathway, it's worth being direct about what the combined Strengths Profile and CliftonStrengths data is telling you — because the launch strategy needs to be *designed around your actual wiring*, not an idealised version of you.

Both assessments converge on the same picture across different frameworks:[^1][^2]

| Cluster | Dominant Strengths | What This Means for Launch |
|---|---|---|
| **Vision & Ideas** | Futuristic, Ideation, Strategic Awareness, Innovation, Incubator | You will generate more frameworks, concepts, and product ideas than you can ever execute. The portfolio you've designed is *exactly* the kind of thing you build. |

### The Critical Insight for Your Launch

You have an almost perfectly configured set of strengths for *designing and launching* this portfolio. You have an equally challenging set of weaknesses for *running and scaling* it. The pathways below are designed explicitly to front-load your strengths and *systematically cover your weaknesses through structure, people, and systems* — not through trying to become someone you're not.

## Your Unique Launch Asset: The RMIT Cryptofinance Classes

Starting next week, you have access to something most founders spend years and significant capital trying to create: a captive audience of approximately 100 motivated, internationally diverse students — across both postgraduate and undergraduate levels — in a subject (Cryptofinance) that sits at the exact intersection of blockchain, tokenomics, and decentralised coordination that Future's Edge is built on.

This is not a marginal resource. It is a potentially transformative launch asset if used deliberately.

**Who these students are:**
- Primarily international students → likely from Southeast Asia, South Asia, East Asia, and other emerging markets — *the exact geographies* identified as primary target markets for the Youth Empowerment programme
- Postgrad students → likely working professionals with industry networks, seeking real-world application of concepts
- Undergrad students → digital natives, comfortable with AI tools, likely to become early adopters and contributors

**What this gives you:**
1. A live research cohort to validate frameworks and tools in real-time
2. A talent pipeline for Future's Edge collective membership (task contributors)
3. A community that can become beta testers, case study generators, and peer advocates
4. Connections back to emerging economy communities for the Youth Programme
5. Academic credibility and co-authorship potential for pre-PhD papers
6. Postgrad students who could take on project management roles (compensating for your Planner weakness)

**The critical design question:** How do you create enough value for them in your role as lecturer that they want to stay connected to Future's Edge beyond the semester — without overcommitting yourself or compromising your academic responsibilities?

## The Four Launch Pathways

Each pathway is designed around a different *primary launch lever*, sequenced to match your strengths and compensate for your weaknesses.

***

## Pathway A: The Classroom-as-Launch-Platform

**Core idea:** Use the RMIT classes as the primary mechanism to co-create Future's Edge's first products — integrating AI for Good content into the Cryptofinance curriculum so students are simultaneously learning *and building the IP*.

### How It Works

**Semester as co-creation sprint:**
- Introduce the AI for Good problem space and Future's Edge vision in Week 1 as the *real-world application context* for the Cryptofinance curriculum
- Frame selected assessment tasks around Future's Edge product lines — e.g., postgrad students design elements of the AI for Good Framework; undergrads research open source tool use cases in their home economies
- Run structured workshops (you're at your best here: Spotlight, Explainer, Catalyst) that double as prototype testing sessions for the Framework products
- Form student teams who voluntarily contribute to the open source tool GitHub repo or framework development as extension activities (not assessed, but recognised and rewarded with Future's Edge tokens)

**By end of Semester 1:**
- You have co-developed V1 of 2-3 core framework documents with student contributions
- You have 10-20 student volunteers who have self-selected as early collective members
- You have 2-3 case study papers started (postgrad research projects) that feed your pre-PhD publication strategy
- You have a waiting list for the Level 1 AI Foundations for Social Impact training (students who want to learn more than the curriculum covers)

**Compensation for your weaknesses:**
- *Persistence:* The semester calendar forces completion. Assessment deadlines are someone else's accountability system — use them.
- *Organiser:* Postgrad students (supervised research project format) manage their own timelines
- *Planner:* The university semester structure IS your project plan — you don't need to create one

**Your professional network role:**
- Guest speakers in class (3-4 per semester) → validates the content, creates connections for students, deepens your network relationships
- Review panel for student framework deliverables → gives professionals a meaningful, low-time-commitment role
- Future advisory board → grows organically from these relationships

**Revenue timeline:** Indirect in Semester 1. By end of Semester 2, training products built from classroom IP start generating direct revenue.

**Biggest risk:** Over-designing the integration and burning out before Week 4. *Mitigation:* Start with one class (the most engaged postgrad group), one AI for Good topic woven in, one optional co-creation activity. Scale only when it's working.

## Pathway B: The Professional Network Sprint

**Core idea:** Launch exclusively with your 10-15 professionals as founding collective members, using a structured 90-day sprint to co-create the first Framework products and the Level 1 Training course.

### How It Works

**Month 1: Collective Formation**
- Host a single half-day in-person or online workshop with the 10-15 professionals (you were born for this: Catalyst, Spotlight, Narrator, Connector)
- Present the Future's Edge AI for Good vision and the five product lines
- Run a rapid session to identify: who wants to contribute to what, what skills they bring, what they want to get out of it
- Establish the task marketplace mechanics — even informally at first: who commits to what by when

**Month 2: First Product Sprint**
- Focus the entire collective on one product: the AI Mission Alignment Canvas (Framework Product Line 2)
- Break into 3-4 working groups, each owning a component
- You facilitate weekly 30-minute review calls (your Explainer + Feedback strengths in action)
- Target: a print-ready, field-testable V1 Canvas by Day 60

**Month 3: Validation and First Revenue**
- Host 2-3 paid half-day workshops with NFPs and social enterprises using the Canvas
- Price: AUD $500–$800/person (or AUD $3,000–$5,000 for a whole team)
- Document learnings and iterate
- First revenue. First case study. First proof of concept.

**RMIT as amplifier, not engine:**
- Introduce the Canvas as a supplementary case study in class
- Invite 2-3 postgrad students to attend (not deliver) one of the paid workshops as observers
- Their feedback enriches the framework; their presence begins the pipeline

**Revenue timeline:** First revenue by Month 3. AUD $15,000–$30,000 by Month 6 from workshops alone if 4-6 sessions are run.

**Compensation for your weaknesses:**
- *Persistence:* The 90-day sprint structure creates external commitment with the professional network as accountability peers — they will keep you honest
- *Planner:* Assign one of the professionals (ideally someone with project management background) as Sprint Facilitator — they own the task tracking
- *Organiser:* Keep the scope ruthlessly narrow. One product. One sprint. One team. The Ideation strength will scream to expand — don't let it.

**Biggest risk:** Your Incubator + Ideation combination will keep the Canvas in "thinking and refining" mode indefinitely. *Mitigation:* Set a hard launch date in Week 1 with the group. "V1 launches on [date], imperfect and real." Publish it publicly on that date regardless.

## Pathway C: The Open Source Community Build

**Core idea:** Launch Future's Edge's AI for Good identity through a GitHub-hosted open source project — one well-designed tool that attracts contributors, builds community credibility, and creates the foundation for everything else.

### How It Works

**The anchor tool:** Start with the **ImpactLens** concept (AI-assisted impact measurement and funder reporting) — this is both the most universally needed tool in the NFP sector and the most demonstrable in a short sprint.

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Design Sprint**
- Write a compelling GitHub README that frames the project: mission, vision, roadmap, contribution guide
- Use your Writer unrealised strength — this is the moment to activate it. A well-written open source README is one of the most powerful community-building documents in tech.
- Post to relevant communities: AI4Good forums, NonprofitAF Slack, Tech To The Rescue, LinkedIn (your personal brand)
- Use the RMIT cryptofinance students to identify blockchain-based data integrity features (direct curriculum connection)

**Phase 2 (Weeks 4-8): First Contributors**
- Your Connector strength activates here — personally recruit 5-10 early contributors from your professional network and RMIT student volunteers
- Run a public online "build session" (2 hours, you facilitate via Zoom): frame the problem, demo a V0 prototype, invite contributions
- Your Spotlight and Catalyst strengths make this kind of session effortless and energising for participants

**Phase 3 (Months 3-6): Community Growth → Revenue**
- As contributors grow, the hosted SaaS version launches (paid, based on the open source core)
- Framework and Training products are developed *by the community* rather than by Pete alone — massively reducing the Persistence and Organiser pressure
- Partnerships with AI4NGO, Tech To The Rescue, and OpenAI for Nonprofits programs are pitched with the community traction as proof of demand

**Revenue timeline:** Slowest of the four pathways (6-12 months to meaningful revenue). But creates the strongest intellectual property and community moat.

**Compensation for your weaknesses:**
- *Persistence:* Open source communities self-perpetuate — contributors create accountability that doesn't rely on Pete's follow-through
- *Organiser:* GitHub is the project management system. Issues, PRs, and milestones structure the work
- *Personal Responsibility:* The community owns deliverables, not Pete

**Biggest risk:** The open source community requires consistent, visible leadership presence in the early months to avoid dying quietly. Your Time Optimiser weakness means this could slip. *Mitigation:* Commit to ONE public action per week (a post, a PR review, a contributor reply). One. Not ten.

## Pathway D: The Revenue-First Sprint

**Core idea:** Ignore the infrastructure, community, and frameworks for now. Launch a single paid training product within 30 days using existing knowledge and your extraordinary natural communication skills — generate early revenue that funds everything else.

### How It Works

**The product:** A 3-hour live online workshop: *"AI for Good: What Your Organisation Needs to Know in 2026"*

- Target audience: NFP and social enterprise leaders and managers (exactly who you know, and who your RMIT network can connect you to)
- Price: AUD $149/person or AUD $799 for a team of up to 6
- Platform: Zoom + a Notion-based resource pack (your Writer strength finally deployed)
- Content: Synthesise everything from the previous three research sessions in this conversation — you already have more material than you need
- Delivery: A Pete Argent-facilitated session. Your Spotlight, Explainer, Narrator, Humour, Catalyst strengths make this a genuinely premium experience.

**Launch in 30 days:**
- Week 1: Write the landing page (Carrd or Squarespace — simple), design the Notion resource pack
- Week 2: Email your professional network directly (10-15 people = 10-15 potential referrals, each with networks)
- Week 3: Post in 3-5 NFP/social enterprise communities online
- Week 4: Deliver first session to whoever signs up (minimum 5 registrants)
- Post-session: Use feedback to iterate → launch Session 2 within 21 days

**RMIT connection:** Offer postgrad students a discounted seat (AUD $49) as research participants. Their sector connections generate word-of-mouth.

**Revenue timeline:** AUD $1,500–$5,000 from first session. AUD $10,000–$25,000 within 90 days if sessions are run fortnightly and audience grows via referral.

**Compensation for your weaknesses:**
- *Persistence:* Each session has a fixed date and registered participants — external commitments force delivery
- *Planner / Organiser:* The entire "product" is a calendar invite and a Zoom link. Minimal infrastructure to manage.
- *Personal Responsibility:* You're not promising a platform or a framework ecosystem — you're promising 3 hours of your time. You can keep that promise.

**Biggest risk:** Commoditisation. A one-off workshop is easy to copy and doesn't build durable IP. *Mitigation:* From Session 1, frame the workshop as "Session 1 of the AI for Good Practitioner Series" — you're building a curriculum, not selling a talk. Capture learner names for the growing community database.

## The Recommended Approach: A Hybrid Stack

Given your specific situation — RMIT starting next week, 10-15 professionals available, strong ideas but weak follow-through infrastructure — the strongest approach is a deliberate hybrid of Pathways D → B → A, sequenced to match your energy cycle and the resources becoming available to you.

### The 90-Day Stack

**Days 1–30: Pathway D (Revenue-First)**
*Deploy your communication strengths immediately. Generate early revenue. Prove the concept.*

- Launch the 3-hour "AI for Good: What Your Organisation Needs to Know in 2026" workshop
- Promote to professional network + relevant LinkedIn communities
- Run first session before the end of Month 1
- Target: AUD $3,000–$5,000 first 30 days. Real proof. Real feedback. Real energy.

**Days 15–45: Pathway B (Professional Network Sprint)**
*While the first workshops are running, activate the professional collective.*

- Host a single 2-hour session with your 10-15 professionals — frame it as "the founding sprint"
- Present the five product lines. Show them the workshop feedback (your early traction is your credibility)
- Assign roles to compensate for your weaknesses: One person takes Operational Coordination. One takes Content QA. One takes Community Management.
- Begin co-creating the AI Mission Alignment Canvas as the first collective output

**Days 30–90: Pathway A (Classroom Integration)**
*Once you have a week or two of RMIT classes under your belt and understand your students, begin the integration deliberately.*

- In Week 3–4 of semester, introduce Future's Edge and AI for Good as the applied context for your curriculum
- Offer a simple, optional co-creation pathway: students who want to contribute get tokens, recognition, and access to the professional network
- By end of Month 3: You have a running workshop series, a founding collective, a first framework product in development, and a student pipeline beginning to form

### Who Owns What (Solving the Weakness Problem)

| Role | Responsibility | Who It Should Be |
|---|---|---|
| **Chief Catalyst** | Vision, relationships, teaching, facilitation, content design | Pete — this is you at your best |
| **Sprint Facilitator** | Task tracking, deadline management, accountability for collective | One professional from your network (ideally a PM or ops background) |
| **Content Steward** | Quality review of frameworks, training materials, open source docs | One professional with sector expertise (NFP or consulting background) |
| **Community Manager** | GitHub, LinkedIn community, student engagement, forum moderation | An engaged postgrad RMIT student (paid in tokens; real responsibility) |
| **Revenue Tracker** | Weekly revenue review, pipeline management, invoicing | You (with a simple dashboard) OR a bookkeeper-level support person |

This is not a big team. It is five clearly defined roles across perhaps 3-4 people. The critical insight from your Strengths Profile is that you are *not* the person to hold yourself accountable — you need external structures and people who will do that for you, without judgment, because that's the design of the system, not a reflection of failure.[^1]

## The One Thing That Matters Most Right Now

Your Activator strength will push you to start five things simultaneously. Your Ideation strength will generate twenty more things you could start. Your Persistence weakness will mean that in three months, most of them are half-finished.

**The single most important decision you can make before your first RMIT class next week is this:**

*Choose ONE product. Run ONE cohort. Deliver ONE completed thing to a REAL customer.*

The AI for Good workshop (Pathway D) is the most achievable version of that in 30 days. Everything else in this portfolio becomes easier once you have the energy, credibility, and evidence of execution that comes from completing something real.

You have the vision. You have the platform. You have the network. You have the students. And you have a set of strengths that are almost perfectly aligned to launching exactly this kind of mission-driven, community-powered, ideas-led portfolio.

The question — as your Strengths Profile has been trying to tell you — is not whether you can design it. It's whether you can *persist through the hard middle parts* to the point where it runs without you holding every thread. That requires building a system and a team that compensates for your genuine weaknesses before the launch energy fades.

Start with the workshop. Build the team. Trust the system.
