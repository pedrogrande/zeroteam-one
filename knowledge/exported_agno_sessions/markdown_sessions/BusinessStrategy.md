# Business strategy

Based on Roger Martin's perspective that "a strategy is an integrative set of choices that positions you on a playing field of your choice in a way that you win," new founders should ask themselves critical questions to define their strategic playing field and the pillars that will support their success.

Here are questions to help new founders, drawing from the provided transcript:

## Defining your playing field

These questions help entrepreneurs choose where their business will compete.

* **Where will we choose to play?**  
  * What specific customer segments or markets will we target?  
  * What geographic areas will we focus on?  
  * Are there playing fields (markets, segments) that we are explicitly choosing *not* to be in? Why?  
  * What makes this chosen playing field attractive and viable for us?

## Defining how to win

These questions focus on how the business will achieve a superior position within the chosen playing field.

* **How will we choose to win in our selected playing field?**  
  * What will be our unique value proposition for customers in this field? How will we be better than anyone else at serving these customers?  
  * What is our theory for how we will achieve a competitive advantage (e.g., lower cost, differentiation, specific niche)?  
  * How will customers perceive our offering as superior to competitors'?  
  * What outcome or competitive position do we aim to achieve?

## Identifying necessary capabilities

This involves understanding the resources and competencies required to execute the strategy.

* **What capabilities must be in place to support our choices of where to play and how to win?**  
  * What specific skills, technologies, assets, or processes do we need to develop or acquire?  
  * Are these capabilities unique or difficult for competitors to replicate?  
  * How do these capabilities align to deliver our chosen way to win?

## Establishing management systems

These questions concern the systems and processes needed to support the strategy and its execution.

* **What management systems are required to build and maintain these capabilities?**  
  * What systems (e.g., for talent development, operations, customer feedback, innovation) need to be established or reinforced?  
  * How will these systems ensure the consistent execution of our strategy?  
  * How will we measure progress and the effectiveness of our strategy?

## Articulating aspirations and goals

This helps to clarify the overall objective the strategy aims to achieve.

* **What is the specific aspiration or goal this strategy is designed to achieve?**  
  * What does success look like for us in quantifiable and qualitative terms?  
  * How does this aspiration connect to our broader vision for the company?

## Laying out the logic and assumptions

Understanding the underlying assumptions is crucial for adapting the strategy as the environment changes.

* **What would have to be true about our company, the industry, our competition, and our customers for this strategy to work?**  
  * What are the key assumptions underpinning our theory of how we will win?  
  * How will we monitor these assumptions and recognize if they are not holding true?  
  * What are the major risks associated with these assumptions?

By addressing these questions, new founders can move beyond mere planning – which focuses on controllable activities and costs – towards developing a true strategy that outlines a coherent set of choices aimed at achieving a specific, competitive outcome in a chosen market . This strategic approach, while potentially causing more initial "angst" due to its inherent uncertainties, gives the business the best possible chance of winning .
