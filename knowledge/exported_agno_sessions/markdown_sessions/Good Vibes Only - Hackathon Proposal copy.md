## The opportunity

From our conversation last week, a few things stood out. 

Natural Velocity is doing something genuinely rare:

* Building a physical home for responsible AI innovation in Melbourne  
* A real commitment to connecting universities to industry and turning research into startups that matter. 

The Innovation Centre isn't just a co-working space; it's the beginning of an ecosystem. And ecosystems need community.

The gap we see is this: 

* Melbourne has no recurring, flagship event where ethical AI isn't just discussed in panels, it's actually *built*.   
* Most hackathons optimise for speed and technical cleverness. None hold participants accountable to responsible AI standards.   
* None deliberately create a pipeline from university talent to industry mentors to commercialisation pathways.   
* And almost none are accessible to the designers, researchers, non-technical founders, and community-minded builders who arguably have the most important perspectives to bring.

The timing couldn't be better. 

* Australia's AI Ethics Principles are now policy.   
* Public interest in AI for Good is at an inflection point.   
* And the rise of vibe coding: AI-assisted development that lets anyone prototype ideas without deep technical skills, has genuinely democratised who can participate in a hackathon. 

***This is the moment to create an event that Melbourne's AI community will talk about, return to, and grow with.***

## Introducing *Good Vibes Only*

**Good Vibes Only** is a two-day ethical AI hackathon preceded by a four-week workshop series, hosted at the Natural Velocity Innovation Centre.

Participants use vibe coding to build working prototypes that address real-world problems. 

They are judged not just on what they build, but on *who it serves*, *how responsibly it does so*, and *whether it has a genuine path to impact and commercialisation*.

The name works on three levels simultaneously:

* **Vibe coding** — the accessible, AI-assisted prototyping method at the heart of the event  
* **Good vibes** — the human-centred, positive-impact intent that every entry must demonstrate  
* **"Only"** — a standard and a commitment; only responsible, human-first AI is welcome here

### What makes this different

Most hackathons are won by the team that was already best equipped to compete. Good Vibes Only is designed from the ground up to change that. 

*Pre-hackathon workshop series*  
The four-week workshop series means participants arrive with the skills, the language, and the confidence to build and pitch well. 

*Judging criteria*  
The judging criteria mean that a brilliant ethical solution built by a mixed team of a designer, a researcher, and a first-time coder can beat a technically impressive but hollow prototype. 

*Post-event incubation pathway*  
The post-event incubation pathway means the best ideas don't disappear into a slide deck, they have somewhere to go.

### Challenge tracks

Participants choose one track when registering:

* **AI for community** — civic tech, accessibility, mental health, sustainability

---

### Judging criteria

| Criterion | What judges assess | Weighting |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Impact potential | Real problem, clear beneficiaries, validated need, meaningful scale | 25% |
| Commercialisation potential | Viable path to market, funding, or scale in the Australian context | 20% |
| Level of innovation | Novel use of AI, creative vibe coding approach, goes beyond the obvious | 20% |
| Responsible AI | Transparent, human-first, inclusive by design, bias considered — *would the people this affects be comfortable with how it was built?* | 20% |
| Pitch and prototype quality | Functional demo, compelling story, clear articulation of who it's for and why it works | 15% |
