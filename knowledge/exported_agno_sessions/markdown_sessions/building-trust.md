# Building Trust in AI

Research and government policy converge on four core pathways to building trust in AI among Australians, grounded in what Australians themselves say they need.

## What Australians Say They Need

The KPMG/University of Melbourne study is direct on this: **83% of Australians say they would be more willing to trust AI if specific assurances were in place**, including: [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/au/en/insights/artificial-intelligence-ai/trust-in-ai-global-insights-2025.html)

- Adherence to international AI standards
- Responsible AI governance practices
- Visible monitoring of system accuracy
- Government and regulator oversight (supported by 80%) [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/au/en/insights/artificial-intelligence-ai/trust-in-ai-global-insights-2025.html)

## 1. Regulation & Clear Rules

This is the single most cited trust-builder: [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/itsjoshphillips_four-companies-will-spend-the-equivalent-activity-7431486172687937536-6gMf)

- **77% of Australians** believe regulation is necessary, and 76% want international laws specifically
- Only 30% believe current laws are adequate — so action, not rhetoric, is what's needed
- Policy recommendations include transparency frameworks for automated decisions, electoral integrity safeguards, and expanding Australia's AI Safety Institute [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/itsjoshphillips_four-companies-will-spend-the-equivalent-activity-7431486172687937536-6gMf)
- The Government's updated **Policy for the Responsible Use of AI in Government** (effective December 2025) introduces mandatory agency transparency statements, accountability measures, and a new AI Review Committee [digital.gov](https://www.digital.gov.au/policy/ai/australian-public-service-ai-plan-2025/trust)

## 2. Transparency & Visible Accountability

Australians distrust what they can't see — so making AI use visible is critical: [dta.gov](https://www.dta.gov.au/articles/responsible-choices-new-policy-using-ai-australian-government)

- Government agencies are now required to publish **public AI transparency statements** explaining *why* they use AI, *how* it is governed, and *how* citizens can challenge decisions [dta.gov](https://www.dta.gov.au/articles/responsible-choices-new-policy-using-ai-australian-government)
- Making AI-generated content labelled and identifiable addresses deepfake-driven fear directly
- Businesses should visibly explain where AI is used in customer interactions and provide a clear human-in-the-loop option and appeals pathway [agile-insights.com](https://www.agile-insights.com.au/australias-trust-in-ai-2025/)

## 3. AI Literacy & Education

Low AI knowledge is a root cause, and Australians are significantly under-educated compared to global peers: [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/au/en/insights/artificial-intelligence-ai/trust-in-ai-global-insights-2025.html)

- Only 24% of Australians have undertaken any AI training (vs. 39% globally) — closing this gap is foundational [kpmg](https://kpmg.com/au/en/insights/artificial-intelligence-ai/trust-in-ai-global-insights-2025.html)
- The **National AI Plan** (released December 2025) commits to workforce AI literacy programs and APS-wide capability uplift [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/australias-new-national-ai-plan-why-matters-beyond-its-mhlanga-ph-d-zguqc)
- Organisations should implement role-based AI training and short internal AI policies — not just top-level briefings, but practical, task-specific guidance [agile-insights.com](https://www.agile-insights.com.au/australias-trust-in-ai-2025/)
- Critically, Minderoo Foundation research found that **when Australians are simply informed** about the AI Safety Institute and National AI Plan's intentions, most shift to believing they could be effective — meaning *awareness itself* moves the needle [minderoo](https://www.minderoo.org/stories/ai-research-feb-2026/)

## 4. Demonstrated, People-First Benefits

The motivational pathway matters too — trust grows when people see AI working for them, not just for productivity metrics: [ai.uq.edu](https://ai.uq.edu.au/project/trust-artificial-intelligence-global-study)

- The APS AI Plan frames its approach explicitly as **"people-first"** — ensuring AI improves public service delivery in ways Australians can directly experience [digital.gov](https://www.digital.gov.au/policy/ai/australian-public-service-ai-plan-2025/trust)
- Highlighting genuine community benefits (faster healthcare, better accessibility, smarter services) rather than abstract economic projections shifts public perception
- Avoiding the "MLM vibes" problem means co-designing AI tools *with* communities rather than deploying them *at* them [ia.acs.org](https://ia.acs.org.au/article/2025/aussies-more-scared-than-excited-about-ai.html)

## The Communication Gap

Perhaps the most actionable near-term finding: only **9% of Australians** have heard of the AI Safety Institute or the National AI Plan — yet most respond positively once they learn what these initiatives do. A large part of Australia's trust deficit is simply an **awareness and communication deficit**, meaning consistent, plain-language public messaging could shift sentiment meaningfully before any major policy changes take full effect. [minderoo](https://www.minderoo.org/stories/ai-research-feb-2026/)
