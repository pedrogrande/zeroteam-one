## 🔑 CROSS-CUTTING OBSERVATIONS

Looking across all 50+ use cases, I see several patterns that directly address your "wall of text" frustration and suggest novel interaction paradigms:

**The "Wall of Text" Problem is Really Five Problems:**
1. **Structure Destruction** — Rich, multi-dimensional information is flattened into linear prose
2. **Extraction Friction** — You can't grab, move, remix, or repurpose components
3. **Progressive Disclosure Failure** — Everything is shown at once or hidden behind "show more"
4. **Provenance Opacity** — You can't see *why* something was said or how confident the AI is
5. **Composition Impossibility** — You can't take outputs and plug them into other workflows

**Meta-Use Cases (What People Are Really Doing):**
- **Thinking** — not just producing outputs, but *changing how they think*
- **Navigating complexity** — exploring possibility spaces, not just getting answers
- **Composing** — combining, recombining, and evolving outputs across sessions
- **Deciding** — seeing trade-offs, not getting recommendations
- **Learning** — building understanding, not just receiving information

These meta-use cases are where the real product opportunity lives — because no existing tool serves them well, and they're what knowledge workers actually spend their time doing.
