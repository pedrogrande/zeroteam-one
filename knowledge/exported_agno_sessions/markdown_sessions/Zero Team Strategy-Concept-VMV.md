# Business Concept

| Key Questions | Your Response |
| :---- | :---- |
| Can you provide an overview of your business concept or idea? | An agent-native social enterprise offering AI training and consulting along with an online membership community. Training can be delivered onsite for organisations, or in instructor-led online webinars for individuals. |
| Who are your target customers or key stakeholders? | Individuals seeking career advancement, small businesses, not-for-profits, and mid-size firms — particularly those navigating AI adoption and future-of-work transitions. |
| What is your business model? | Fees for training (onsite & online), consulting engagements, job board listings, and community subscriptions. |
| Which markets or regions are you planning to operate in? | Melbourne initially, scaling nationwide and internationally via online delivery. |
| Who are your main competitors, and what differentiates you? | Existing consulting firms, training providers, YouTube influencers, and online-only platforms. Differentiated by deep expertise in trustworthy AI, agentic system design frameworks, and 20+ years of innovative technology education delivery — offering a more human-centered, practitioner-led perspective. |
| What are your short-term and long-term goals? | **Short-term:** Launch training programs and consulting services in Melbourne; build an online membership community; deliver onsite and webinar-based AI literacy workshops. **Long-term:** Scale to a nationwide and international audience; develop a recognised brand in trustworthy and agentic AI education; build a self-sustaining community platform with recurring revenue. |
| What are the major risks or uncertainties? | Rapidly evolving AI landscape making curriculum quickly outdated; market saturation from well-funded competitors; difficulty differentiating in a crowded online education space; over-reliance on founder as sole subject-matter expert; regulatory uncertainty around AI in the workplace. |
| What partnerships, suppliers, or stakeholders are critical to your success? | Universities (e.g. RMIT) for credibility and talent pipelines; industry partners for real-world case studies; corporate clients for onsite training contracts; not-for-profit networks; AI tool vendors (e.g. platform providers); government agencies and workforce development bodies. |
| What is your vision for the business's impact on its industry or community? | To democratise access to practical, trustworthy AI education — empowering individuals and organisations to adopt AI confidently, ethically, and effectively. To model what an "agent-native" social enterprise looks like, and to help build a future workforce that uses AI to augment human capability rather than replace it. |

## Mission

| Question | Response |
| :---- | :---- |
| What problem does the organization exist to solve? | Most people and organisations are either ignoring AI out of fear, or adopting it recklessly without understanding the risks. There is a critical gap between AI hype and practical, trustworthy AI implementation — and most training providers aren't filling it \[^2\_2\]\[^2\_4\]. |
| Who does the organization serve? | Individuals seeking career advancement in an AI-driven economy; small businesses and not-for-profits navigating AI adoption; and mid-size firms needing practical consulting on trustworthy, agentic AI systems \[^2\_4\]. |
| What is the organization's main activity or focus? | Designing and delivering practical AI education and consulting that builds genuine capability — not just awareness. This includes onsite and online training, consulting on agentic system design, and an online community for ongoing learning and peer support \[^2\_4\]\[^2\_3\]. |
| What measurable outcomes does the organization aim to achieve? | Learners who can immediately apply AI tools in their work; organizations with a clear, actionable AI adoption roadmap; a growing community with measurable engagement and career outcomes; and consulting engagements with verifiable improvements in how clients design and govern AI systems \[^2\_6\]\[^2\_3\]. |

## Values

| Question | Response |
| :---- | :---- |
| What beliefs or principles are non-negotiable? | AI must augment human thinking, not replace it. Learning must build genuine confidence, not just checkbox compliance. Trustworthiness is structural, not just declarative — it must be designed in, not bolted on \[^2\_2\]\[^2\_3\]. |
| What does the organization stand for? Or against? | **For:** Human-centered AI, practical education, cognitive agency, equity of access, and profitable impact. **Against:** AI hype without substance, declarative ethics without structural enforcement, training that creates dependency rather than capability, and education that leaves people behind \[^2\_2\]\[^2\_3\]. |
| What kind of work environment does it want to create? | One where curiosity is rewarded, questions are welcomed over answers, and the facilitator's job is to inspire — not just inform. Peer-to-peer knowledge sharing is a design feature, not an afterthought. Fun is a legitimate learning tool \[^2\_3\]. |
| What is the right way to treat customers, employees, and stakeholders? | With genuine care for their outcomes, not just their satisfaction. Meet people where they are. Build their confidence before you build their skills. Be transparent about what AI can and cannot do. Share knowledge freely — the rising tide lifts all boats \[^2\_1\]\[^2\_3\]. |
| What kind of legacy does the organization want to leave? | A generation of practitioners who adopted AI thoughtfully, ethically, and effectively — and who became advocates for a more trustworthy AI ecosystem in their own workplaces and communities \[^2\_2\]\[^2\_1\]. |
