# Jobs to Be Done

The JTBD framework asks: *what is Alex actually hiring a solution to do?* — not what features they want, but what progress they're trying to make in their situation. There are three layers: functional, emotional, and social jobs.

## Functional Jobs

*The practical tasks Alex needs to get done*

- **"Help me build a governance framework"** — create a defensible AI policy that cascades from leadership to frontline staff without requiring every principal or trainer to invent their own [^1]
- **"Upskill my people fast"** — give teachers, trainers, and lecturers enough AI literacy to feel confident and act consistently, without pulling them out of delivery for weeks [^1]
- **"Fix the academic integrity problem"** — replace blunt detection tools with an approach that's educationally sound, legally defensible, and that staff will actually use [^1]
- **"Reduce my compliance exposure"** — map AI-related risks to the specific regulatory obligations my institution carries (ASQA, TEQSA, Privacy Act, child safety) before an auditor or incident does it for me [^1]
- **"Free up my team's time"** — automate high-volume, low-value work (learner support queries, compliance documentation, marking) so staff focus on what only humans can do [^1]


## Emotional Jobs

*How Alex wants to feel — and stop feeling*

- **Stop feeling exposed** — the current state of "no clear policy, inconsistent practice" is genuinely anxiety-inducing for someone responsible for institutional reputation [^1]
- **Feel like a leader, not a laggard** — Alex doesn't want to be first, but doesn't want to be last either; they want to move *thoughtfully*, which is a form of pride [^1]
- **Feel confident in conversations** — with the board, with parents, with staff, with regulators; right now those conversations are harder than they should be [^1]
- **Stop being pitched at** — Alex is tired of vendors who see their institution as a sales target; they want to feel understood, not prospected [^1]


## Social Jobs

*How Alex wants to be seen by others*

- **Be seen as the person who got AI right** — the leader who built something their institution will point to in five years as the foundation, not the cautionary tale [^1]
- **Be a credible voice in the sector** — Wesley wants to set the independent school standard; CEM wants to model how a large system leads [^1]; that's not ego, it's professional identity
- **Be trusted by their community** — parents, students, teachers, and regulators need to believe their institution is handling AI responsibly, and Alex is the face of that [^1]


## The Hiring Criteria

*What would make Alex "hire" Zero Team over alternatives?*

- **Sector fluency** — speaks education regulation, not just AI; knows what ASQA audits feel like, what TEQSA expects, what child safety actually means in practice [^1]
- **Values alignment** — can engage with a Catholic social tradition, a Uniting Church identity, or an international student welfare obligation without needing it explained [^1]
- **Educator credibility** — has delivered in classrooms and training rooms, not just consulted about them [^1]
- **Speed to value** — can show something real quickly; Alex needs momentum, not a six-month discovery phase [^1]
- **Not a vendor** — the co-design framing matters; Alex doesn't want to buy a product, they want a partner who shares accountability for the outcome [^1]

# Value Proposition

Using the classic **Jobs → Pains → Gains** structure, synthesised into a statement Zero Team can actually use:

## The Core Statement

> **For education leaders navigating AI without a trustworthy partner, Zero Team is the educator-led AI capability firm that helps institutions build governance frameworks, upskill their people, and deploy responsible AI solutions — designed specifically for the regulatory, values, and student-welfare context of Australian education.**

> Unlike technology-first vendors who import corporate AI tools into education, Zero Team brings decades of frontline education delivery, deep sector regulatory knowledge, and a principled approach to trustworthy AI — so leaders can move confidently, not just quickly.

## Tagline Options

Three directions, depending on what Zero Team wants to lead with:

- **Credibility-first:** *"The AI partner that speaks education."*
- **Safety-first:** *"Trustworthy AI, built for the people who teach."*
- **Outcome-first:** *"AI done right — from the classroom out."*

The strongest is probably the credibility-first line — it directly addresses Alex's biggest frustration (vendors who don't understand education) and positions Zero Team's identity as the differentiator, not a feature list.
